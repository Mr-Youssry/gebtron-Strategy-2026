# 02 — The World Around Us

---

## 2.1 European Machinery Sector

The global machinery industry is worth EUR 3.26T (2023). Germany declined 9% in 2024. A EUR 124B investment gap has opened between Germany and the US. 74% of European OEMs cite localization as "highly relevant."

**What this means for Gebtron:** European partners need regional growth channels more than ever. Gebtron's integrator model becomes more valuable as European domestic markets contract.

## 2.2 The Circular Economy Opportunity

| Metric | Value |
|--------|-------|
| Annual value lost to MSW globally | USD 90-120 billion |
| Global circularity rate | 7% |
| Middle East cement (2024 → 2030) | 184M → 279M tonnes (6.98% CAGR) |
| Thermal substitution rate — Europe | 52% |
| Thermal substitution rate — MENA | <10% |
| Thermal substitution rate — Africa | <5% |

The gap between Europe's 52% and MENA/Africa's <10% is Gebtron's entire addressable market for alternative fuels equipment and EAAS.

## 2.3 The EAAS Revolution

Equipment-as-a-Service grows at **35% annually**. The shift from CapEx to OPEX is structural.

| Metric | Direct Sale | EAAS Model |
|--------|-------------|------------|
| Investment | ~USD 3M | ~USD 3M |
| One-time profit | ~USD 350K | — |
| Annual revenue | — | ~USD 2M |
| Total profit (15 years) | USD 350K | **~USD 15M (43x)** |

EAAS doubles the addressable market: factories that can't afford $3M CapEx can afford $50K/month.

---

## 2.4 The Talent Crisis

62% of manufacturers cite talent as their #1 challenge. 80% lack Industry 4.0 skills. 24% of the workforce is aged 55+ and retiring. 83% expect it to worsen.

**The dual effect:** Gebtron struggles to hire (5-6 month cycles, Egyptian brain drain), BUT clients also lack talent — creating demand for turnkey solutions and managed services that only local-presence companies can provide.

---

## 2.5 Saudi Vision 2030

HFO subsidy removal at 22%/year toward 2030. EAAS economics clear by ~2027. Mining growing (Aramco subsidiaries). Slow decision-making, but enormous opportunity.

---

## 2.6 European Succession Wave

24% of the European manufacturing workforce is aged 55+. In Germany alone, ~600,000 SMEs plan succession within 5 years — 47% have NO identified successor. Government-backed financing (KfW + Bürgschaftsbank) enables 10% buyer equity acquisitions.
