# 07 — OKRs (Q3 2026 – Q1 2027)

> [!warning] Draft v0.1
> This document is a working draft. All sections are under review and will be finalized after the June 2026 strategy bootcamp at AFW Alexandria.

---

## How These OKRs Were Built

```
Strategy Paper (01-06)
        ↓
SWOT Analysis (09)
        ↓
TOWS Matrix — 23 strategic actions
        ↓
Priority Tiers (Critical → High → Medium)
        ↓
OKRs by Quarter (Tier 1 → Q3, Tier 2 → Q4, Tier 3 → Q1 2027)
```

Each objective below includes:
- **TOWS Link** — which strategic action(s) it executes
- **Horizon** — H1 (core), H2 (emerging), or foundational
- **Owner** — who drives it (to be confirmed at bootcamp)

## Q3 2026 (Jul–Sep) — Tier 1: Critical Actions

*Q3 starts after the June-July bootcamp. These objectives execute the five critical priorities identified in the TOWS analysis.*

---

### Objective 1: Launch EAAS with First Commercial Engagement

**TOWS Link:** SO-1 (engineering quality + EAAS growth) + ST-2 (lock clients with contracts)
**Horizon:** H2 — Build & Scale
**Owner:** CEO + Consultant

| Key Result | Target | Measurable |
|------------|--------|------------|
| KR1.1 | Deliver EAAS Excel financial model v3 (validated by Mohamed and Abdel Rahman) | Model accepted and usable for client proposals |
| KR1.2 | Submit EAAS proposal to at least 1 cement factory (UAE C&D pathway or Egypt MSW) | Proposal document delivered to client |
| KR1.3 | Sign at least 1 waste supply agreement (C&D or MSW) | Signed agreement in hand |
| KR1.4 | Complete SPV legal template for UAE EAAS entity | Legal template reviewed and approved |

**Why Q3:** EAAS is the center of gravity of the entire strategy (SO-1). The C&D waste pathway (Al Dhafra-Fujairah, 1.57-year equity payback) offers a faster route than MSW. The financial model (v2 in progress) should be finalized by bootcamp; v3 incorporates feedback.

**Risk trigger:** If no proposal submitted by end of Q3 → review value proposition and adjust approach (Scenario B from risk register).

---

### Objective 2: Establish Financial Leadership

**TOWS Link:** WO-1 (CFO unlocks EAAS) + WT-5 (financial function before crisis) — *appears in 3 TOWS quadrants, making it the highest-leverage single action*
**Horizon:** Foundational — enables all horizons
**Owner:** CEO

| Key Result | Target | Measurable |
|------------|--------|------------|
| KR2.1 | Define CFO role requirements and ideal candidate profile | Job description approved by Mohamed |
| KR2.2 | Launch CFO recruitment (external search or fractional CFO engagement) | Active candidate pipeline or fractional CFO contracted |
| KR2.3 | Establish monthly financial reporting cadence (as interim measure) | First monthly report produced |
| KR2.4 | Complete UDO ERP implementation to operational status | ERP producing usable reports |

**Why Q3:** The CFO gap blocks EAAS deal structuring (WO-1), financial reporting (WT-5), and investor readiness. Every quarter without financial leadership is a quarter where EAAS deals cannot be properly structured. The monthly reporting cadence is a bridge until the CFO is in place.

---

### Objective 3: Begin Building Management Depth

**TOWS Link:** WT-1 (management depth before crisis) + WT-2 (formalize HR before poaching) + WO-5 (promote from within)
**Horizon:** Foundational — survival priority
**Owner:** CEO + HR (new hire)

| Key Result | Target | Measurable |
|------------|--------|------------|
| KR3.1 | New HR person (started April 15) fully onboarded and operational | HR handling recruitment independently |
| KR3.2 | Net +2 engineers hired in Q3 | 2 engineers productive by end of Q3 |
| KR3.3 | Design career band framework (E1-E5 technical, M1-M4 management) | Framework document approved |
| KR3.4 | Identify 1 internal candidate for promotion to team lead role | Candidate named and development plan started |

**Why Q3:** Key-person risk (WT-1) is the single most dangerous vulnerability. Building even one layer of management between Mohamed and the engineering team reduces this risk. The career band framework (from The Gebtron Way) enables the promote-from-within model that's essential for a company that can't hire fast enough externally.

## Open Items for Bootcamp

> These OKRs are the analyst's proposal based on TOWS analysis. They require Mohamed's validation at the June bootcamp.

### Decisions Required

- **EAAS first target:** UAE (C&D pathway) or Egypt (MSW pathway)? Both have merits — C&D is faster, MSW is larger. (Affects O1, O5, O9)
- **CFO: external hire vs. fractional?** Full-time CFO is ideal but takes 3-6 months. Fractional CFO could start immediately. (Affects O2)
- **European expansion: acquire or build organic?** Research (Q3) produces options; Q4 requires a decision. (Affects O6)
- **Saudi timing: still H2 priority?** Subsidy timeline uncertain. Should Saudi entity setup proceed in Q1 2027 or defer? (Affects O10)
- **ESOP pool size and terms** — 10% or 15%? 3 or 4 year vesting? Directly impacts retention power. (Affects O7, O11)
- **Revenue targets in absolute numbers:** Current targets are relative (+30%, +100%). Need 2025 actuals to convert. (Affects O8, O9)

### Missing Data

- **2025 revenue baseline** — finance team / QuickBooks update
- **Current headcount by role** — HR
- **Project profitability data** — which projects are profitable and which aren't
- **European acquisition targets research** — Ahmed (committed March 26)
- **EAAS financial model v2/v3** — Ahmed's deliverable
- **Africa market research** — desk research for geographic expansion
