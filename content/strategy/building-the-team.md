## 4.2 The Gebtron Way — Promote from Within

Inspired by P&G's discipline (via Ahmed's wife, 9 years at P&G) and Austrian family business values (Kockele visit), synthesized into a hybrid model:

| Dimension | P&G | Austrian Family | **The Gebtron Way** |
|-----------|-----|-----------------|---------------------|
| Hiring mix | 90% entry | Hire for fit | 40% experienced / 30% mid / 30% entry |
| Turnover | Accepted | Low by design | <15% target |
| Career path | Management only | Informal | Dual track: management + technical |
| Internal promotion | 99% | Relationship-based | 70%+ internal (after Year 5) |

**Explicitly rejected:** "Up or Out," forced ranking, Day 1 unsupervised client exposure, rapid rotation.

## 4.4 Engineering Talent Pipeline

The Alexandria team is the company's core asset. Mohamed's comparison: a Japanese subsidiary with a 30x40m hall of engineers produces less per engineer than AFW's small team.

**The Egyptian Talent Crisis:**
- Brain drain to the Gulf
- 5-6 month average hiring cycles
- CV inflation and salary chaos
- Alexandria disadvantage vs. Cairo

**Proposed solution:** Target Egyptian expats in the Gulf — offer dual-location model (Gulf salary + Alexandria home base).

## 4.5 Key-Person Risk

Mohamed is simultaneously CEO, chief engineer, lead sales, primary relationship holder, strategy driver, and cultural anchor.

> "I don't have the middle management that will allow me to free my mind to think strategically. I'm still in the trenches."
> — Mohamed Youssef

**Key hires needed:** General Manager for AFW, Head of Engineering, Head of Staff, CFO, HR Manager.

**Mitigation:** ESOP for retention, knowledge documentation (80% target), career bands (E1-E5 technical, M1-M4 management), overcapacity hiring.

## 4.6 ESOP & Retention

| Parameter | Value |
|-----------|-------|
| Total pool | 10-15% of company equity |
| Vesting | 4 years |
| Structure | Options (not direct shares) |
| Liquidity event | Eventual IPO on Dubai exchange |

Three tiers: Base (all employees, phantom shares), Key Talent (senior engineers, additional options), Leadership (directors, larger grants).

## 4.7 The Gebtron Academy (Planned)

**Year 1-2:** Formalize mentoring (Shadow → Assist → Own Supervised → Own Independent). Design career bands. Establish 3 university relationships.

**Year 3-5:** Launch Academy modules. Cross-entity rotation. Produce E4/E5 technical authorities.

**Year 5-10:** Regional expansion using "carriers" — experienced people who embed in new offices for 3-5 years. Alumni network as business development tool.
