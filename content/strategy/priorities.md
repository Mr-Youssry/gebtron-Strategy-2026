## 6.1 Horizon 1: Defend & Extend (0-2 years)

**Core:** Bulk material handling equipment for cement/mining. Egypt (brownfield), UAE (greenfield).

### Egypt Market
22 cement factories, 39 cement lines, 2-3 new/upgrade lines per year. Gebtron is one of only 2 companies installing offline firing technology.

### H1 Financial Baseline (Gebtron FZCO — Audited 2024)

### EAAS: The Central H2 Bet

Clients can manage USD 50K/month but not USD 1M+ CapEx. EAAS removes the barrier: zero upfront cost, guaranteed savings, 15-year contract. Market expands from 5 to 10 opportunities/year, Gebtron share from 40% to 70-90%.

### Saudi Arabia Entry

HFO subsidy removal at 22%/year. Model not profitable at current subsidy levels but clears by ~2027. SIRC partnership opportunity: 5 t/h sludge + 15 t/h RDF.

### AI & Data Analytics (Moved from H3 to H2)

Already active: AI-assisted strategy, transcription, financial modeling. Near-term: client-facing analytics within EAAS contracts. The EAAS model creates the data pipeline AI needs.

### Africa — The Greenfield Opportunity

Research has identified 6 priority markets with a scoring matrix. Key findings:
- Africa's thermal substitution rate is <5% vs. Europe's 52%
- **Zero EAAS competitors** in Sub-Saharan Africa
- CFA Franc zone (Senegal, Côte d'Ivoire) eliminates currency risk (EUR-pegged)
- Bamburi Cement in Kenya has achieved 98% alternative fuel rate — proof of concept
- Dangote's $1B Sinoma deal for 12 new plants excludes AF systems — massive pipeline

Recommended phasing: CFA Zone first (2027-28), then Nigeria (2028-29), then East Africa (2029+).
