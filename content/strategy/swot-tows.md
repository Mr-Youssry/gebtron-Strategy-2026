## TOWS Strategic Matrix

### Tier 1: Critical (Do Now — Q3 2026)

| Priority | TOWS | Action |
|----------|------|--------|
| **Launch EAAS** | SO-1 | First commercial contract. Center of gravity of the strategy. |
| **Hire CFO** | WO-1, WT-5 | Appears in 3 TOWS quadrants. Without financial leadership, EAAS cannot be structured. |
| **Build management depth** | WT-1, WT-2 | Key-person risk is the single most dangerous vulnerability. |
| **Win on service, not price** | ST-1 | Strategic discipline: refuse to enter price wars with Chinese competitors. |
| **Lock clients with EAAS** | ST-2 | First-mover contracts create 15-year switching costs. |

### Tier 2: High (Q3-Q4 2026)

| Priority | TOWS | Action |
|----------|------|--------|
| European expansion path | WO-2, SO-3 | Research and decide: acquire, partner, or build. Succession wave won't last. |
| Circular economy positioning | SO-2 | Position as regional circular economy leader through EAAS + alt fuels. |
| Gebtron Academy launch | WO-4, WO-5 | Formalize talent pipeline. Can't grow without people. |
| ESOP implementation | ST-5 | Retention mechanism before team becomes a poaching target. |
| Focused sequencing | WT-3 | Egypt → UAE → Saudi → Africa. Don't open new fronts until current ones staffed. |

### Tier 3: Medium (Q4 2026 — Q1 2027)

| Priority | TOWS | Action |
|----------|------|--------|
| Saudi market entry | SO-4 | Legal entity, exhibitions, relationship development. Economics clear by 2027. |
| AI within EAAS | SO-5, WO-6 | Client-facing analytics after first installation produces data. |
| Kockele cultivation | SO-6 | Continue relationship-building. Cement mill project is next test. |
| Super RDF development | WT-4 | Technology differentiation for waste supply. |
