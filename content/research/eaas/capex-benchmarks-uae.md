## Capacity Specifications

### Capacity Units & Definitions

| Term | Definition |
|------|------------|
| **t/h** | Tonnes per hour (throughput rate) |
| **t/d** or **TPD** | Tonnes per day (daily capacity) |
| **t/a** or **TPA** | Tonnes per annum (annual capacity) |

### Standard Operating Assumptions

| Parameter | Single Shift | Double Shift | Triple Shift |
|-----------|--------------|--------------|--------------|
| Operating hours/day | 8 | 16 | 24 |
| Operating days/year | 300 | 300 | 330 |
| Utilization factor | 80% | 75% | 70% |
| Effective hours/year | 1,920 | 3,600 | 5,544 |

### Minimum Viable Capacity

Based on industry data and economic analysis: [^1] [^2] [^3]

| Scale | Capacity (t/day) | Capacity (t/h)* | Economic Viability |
|-------|-----------------|-----------------|-------------------|
| **Pilot/Test** | 10-25 | 1-3 | Not economical for commercial operation |
| **Small Scale** | 50-100 | 5-12 | Marginal economics; requires high gate fees |
| **Medium Scale** | 100-300 | 12-20 | Economically viable with gate fees |
| **Optimal Scale** | 300-500 | 20-40 | Best economies of scale [^3] |
| **Large Scale** | 500-1,000 | 40-80 | Industrial scale; cement plant integration |
| **Mega Scale** | 1,000+ | 80+ | World-class facilities (UAE Emirates RDF, Jakarta) |

*Based on 8-hour single shift operation

**Minimum Recommended Capacity for UAE:**
- **Commercial viability threshold:** 100 t/day (with gate fees) [^2]
- **Optimal starting point:** 200-300 t/day [^3]

### Capacity Stepping (Increments)

RDF plant capacity is determined by the **shredder throughput**, which is the bottleneck equipment. Shredders are manufactured in standard capacity increments: [^4] [^5]

**Shredder Capacity Steps:**

| Shredder Capacity (t/h) | Single Shift (t/day) | Double Shift (t/day) | Triple Shift (t/day) | Annual @ 75% Util. (t/a) |
|------------------------|---------------------|---------------------|---------------------|-------------------------|
| **5** | 40 | 80 | 120 | 10,800 |
| **10** | 80 | 160 | 240 | 21,600 |
| **12** | 96 | 192 | 288 | 25,920 |
| **15** | 120 | 240 | 360 | 32,400 |
| **20** | 160 | 320 | 480 | 43,200 |
| **25** | 200 | 400 | 600 | 54,000 |
| **30** | 240 | 480 | 720 | 64,800 |
| **40** | 320 | 640 | 960 | 86,400 |
| **50** | 400 | 800 | 1,200 | 108,000 |
| **60** | 480 | 960 | 1,440 | 129,600 |
| **80** | 640 | 1,280 | 1,920 | 172,800 |

**Standard Capacity Steps (Double Shift Operation):**

```
Minimum → 80 → 160 → 240 → 320 → 400 → 500 → 640 → 800 → 1,000 t/day
Step increment: ~80 t/day (based on 5 t/h shredder increments)
```

**For 1,000 t/day Capacity:**
- Requires ~60-65 t/h total shredding capacity
- Typically achieved with multiple parallel lines (e.g., 3× 20 t/h lines)
- Triple shift operation (24 hrs) with 40-45 t/h can also achieve 1,000 t/day

> **Key Insight:** You cannot specify arbitrary capacities like 100 t/day or 150 t/day without either:
> - Under-sizing equipment (creating bottlenecks)
> - Over-sizing equipment (wasting CAPEX)
>
> Optimal design matches shredder capacity to target throughput.

### Economies of Scale

```
CAPEX per t/day = Base_Cost × (Target_Capacity / Base_Capacity)^Scale_Factor

Where:
- Base_Cost: €20,000 per t/day (at 80 t/day)
- Scale_Factor: 0.7 (typical for process equipment)

Example:
- 200 t/day: €20,000 × (200/80)^0.7 = €20,000 × 1.84 = €36,800 total per t/day
  Actual: €15,500 per t/day (economies of scale achieved)
```
