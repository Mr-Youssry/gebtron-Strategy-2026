### Karl-Friedrich Machan — Founder (1st Generation)

Karl-Friedrich Machan established his engineering business in Augsburg in the early 1970s, focusing on bulk material handling technology. In 1993, he relocated the company to Lunzenau in Saxony, where it has operated ever since under the GöMa brand. He stepped back from operational management in 2022 when his son took over. No LinkedIn profile is publicly findable — consistent with the profile of a retired eastern German engineer of his generation.

## Products

GöMa designs and manufactures **custom conveying and control technology** for bulk materials. Their speciality is **continuous weighing and dosing** — the precision measurement and controlled feeding of bulk solids into industrial processes.

### Core capabilities

| Product / System | Description |
|-----------------|-------------|
| **Continuous weighing & dosing** | Real-time gravimetric control of bulk material feed rates — the precision layer between storage and process |
| **Chain bucket elevators** | Mechanical vertical conveying of bulk solids |
| **Trough belt conveyors** | Horizontal bulk conveying |
| **Pneumatic conveying systems** | Air-based transport for powders and granules |
| **Mixing systems** | Blending of bulk material streams |
| **Process automation** | Control systems for conveying and dosing sequences |

Stone / earth · Plastics · Recycling · Food

## Why GöMa Is the Right Fit for Gebtron

### The missing link in a Pfeiffer mill integration

When Gebtron supplies and installs a Gebr. Pfeiffer vertical roller mill for a cement plant, raw material must be dosed with **precision and consistency** before entering the mill. Today, Gebtron sources that weighing/dosing module from third-party suppliers. GöMa can supply it — with German engineering, CE certification, and fully custom design.

The system integration story becomes: **Gebtron bundles Pfeiffer mill + GöMa dosing + AFW-fabricated structure + Gebtron commissioning as a single EPC package.** This eliminates a procurement step for the client, improves margin for Gebtron, and gives GöMa a channel into MENA cement markets they cannot reach alone.

### Technology gap filled

| Gap Today | How GöMa Fills It |
|-----------|-------------------|
| Gebtron lacks CE-certified weighing/dosing IP | GöMa holds this technology |
| AFW engineers can fabricate but not design control systems | GöMa provides the control engineering |
| Pfeiffer mill package requires raw feed dosing | GöMa integrates seamlessly upstream of the mill |

### Scale match

GöMa is small enough that Gebtron's MENA pipeline represents a **meaningful percentage of their revenue**. This creates real leverage — both in commercial terms (Gebtron as anchor client) and in eventual equity discussions. A EUR 2M annual flow of MENA dosing system orders to a EUR 2M/year company is transformative.

## Deal Structures to Consider

| Structure | How it Works | Why Suitable |
|-----------|-------------|--------------|
| **Distribution partnership** | Gebtron becomes GöMa's exclusive MENA distributor | Lowest friction; builds relationship and revenue track record before any equity conversation |
| **Minority stake (20–30%)** | Gebtron injects EUR 300–500K for growth capital | Gives Gebtron a seat at the table; GöMa gets expansion capital for a sales person or second engineer |
| **Earn-in to majority** | Gebtron buys 20% now, earns additional % for hitting revenue targets together | Aligns incentives; avoids overpaying for a business whose value depends on Jürgen's motivation |
| **Full acquisition** | Gebtron acquires 100% via KfW financing | Clean but only appropriate once relationship is established and Jürgen is ready to exit |

**Recommended sequence:** Distribution partnership (Year 1–2) → Minority stake (Year 2–3) → Earn-in to majority (Year 4–5).
