# WEF — Future of Jobs + Industry 4.0

**Source(s):** WEF Future of Jobs Report 2025 (Insight Report, January 2025); WEF Global Lighthouse Network (2025 cohort, Jan + Sep 2025 press releases, McKinsey co-authored)
**Last verified:** 2026-04-23
**Purpose:** Act I slide 5 (the labour-market backdrop for "why now?") + Act III implications (what Gebtron must do) in the AI-Augmented Engineer deck
**Reading time:** ~6 min

---

## Read when
You need a single, source-cited slide that says: "the work of the engineer is being rewritten; here is what the world's best factories already do." Use for the leadership briefing and the AFW all-hands.

## Key takeaway
By 2030 the WEF expects a **net +78 million jobs globally but 39% of current skills disrupted** — the winners won't be those who hire more engineers, but those who re-skill existing ones around AI + analytics. The 201-site Global Lighthouse Network shows it already works: **+40% labour productivity, –48% lead time, –41% defects** at plants where AI is embedded in daily operations.

## Predictions for engineering, design, production roles

1. **Environmental & Renewable Energy Engineers enter the top-15 fastest-growing jobs.** The "green transition" is the single largest structural driver cited, alongside AI. Demand pulled by energy-efficiency retrofits — directly relevant to bulk-handling feeding lines, conveyors, dedusting. (FoJ 2025, Ch. 2)

2. **"AI-and-Machine-Learning Specialists" grow +82% while "AI Engineers / Domain Solution Architects" become a distinct hiring category.** The report explicitly calls out the move away from pure data scientists toward engineers who *embed* AI into physical operations — the exact profile AFW's design team can re-skill into.

3. **Graphic Designers and Legal Secretaries appear for the first time on the declining list**, flagged specifically because of generative-AI task automation. The implication for mechanical/process design: *drafting is being commoditised; judgement and systems-thinking are not.* (FoJ 2025, Ch. 2, Fig. 2.4)

4. **Construction Workers (+7.0M) and Electrotechnology Engineers remain net-positive** globally. Heavy-industry EPC work is growing, not shrinking — but the *value share* captured by each engineer rises only if AI is used to compress design cycles. (FoJ 2025, Ch. 2)

5. **A "collaborative" 30% of tasks now done jointly by humans + machines** (vs. 47% purely human, 22% purely machine). WEF explicitly frames the future as "human-led, AI-enabled teams" — matching the deck's thesis that the engineer merges with an AI colleague rather than being replaced by one. (FoJ 2025, Ch. 1)

## Lighthouse Network — exemplars

The **Global Lighthouse Network** is a WEF/McKinsey-curated list of factories that have scaled 4th-Industrial-Revolution tech beyond pilots. As of **17 September 2025, 201 sites globally**; the latest cohort shows **50% of top use-cases are now AI/generative-AI enabled** (vs. ~10% in 2022).

**1. China Resources Building Materials Technology — Tianyang cement plant (China).** The only cement plant in the Network. Deployed **30+ 4IR use cases**: advanced analytics, autonomous kiln-trucks, IIoT sensors on the pyro-process. Results: improved energy, labour and equipment efficiency across the grinding + clinker line. **Direct analogue for Gebtron:** bulk-handling feeding-lines + dedusting sit in the same operational envelope.

**2. Qatar Shell GTL — Ras Laffan (Qatar, added Sep 2025).** Gas-to-liquids mega-site; deployed AI-based structural-integrity assessment and frontline worker empowerment tools. Results: **+9% throughput, 99% reliability, –7% emissions.** Relevant as a MENA-region heavy-process analogue (Gebtron's petrochem customer base).

**3. Tüpraş — İzmit refinery (Türkiye, added Sep 2025).** Oil refining + downstream logistics. AI-driven forecasting + yard optimisation. Results: **delivery reliability 85% → 95%, truck-loading time –75%.** Demonstrates that bulk/liquid material-handling logistics — not just the reactor — is where AI pays back fastest.

**4. Schneider Electric Distribution Center — Evreux (France, Sustainability Lighthouse 2025).** Circular-economy distribution node. Single-use plastic –40%, energy –18%. Relevant as a European case for the merger-entity pitch.

Cross-cohort economics (FoJ 2025 + GLN 2025 press): **+40% labour productivity, –48% lead time, –41% defects, –28% energy, –44% cycle time.**

## Macro implications for Gebtron/AFW

- **The work mix is the lever, not headcount.** Gebtron does not need to hire AI specialists; it needs to reskill its ~30 AFW engineers so 30% of their task-mix is AI-collaborative by 2027. That matches WEF's "human-led, AI-enabled" baseline and is the single cheapest way to defend margin.
- **Cement/bulk is a white-space in the Lighthouse map.** Only *one* cement plant worldwide holds the badge. If Gebtron + GECOS position the merged entity as "the AI-native feeding-line integrator for MENA/Europe cement and petchem," the addressable-market narrative is credible on day one.
- **The skills Gebtron already has (systems thinking, creative problem-solving, environmental stewardship) are exactly the ones rising** (FoJ Fig. 3.3). The gap is technological literacy + AI/big-data fluency — which is a training budget of EUR 30–50K, not a hiring line.
- **The declining-skills list is a warning, not a threat.** Drafting, routine QC, clerical coordination are all inside AFW today. Reallocating those hours to AI-augmented design is a +20–30% capacity win without headcount change — the same productivity delta Lighthouses post.
