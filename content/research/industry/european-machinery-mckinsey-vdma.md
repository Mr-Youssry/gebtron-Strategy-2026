# How to Succeed: Strategic Options for European Machinery

**Source:** McKinsey & Company / VDMA Joint Study (2015)
**Pages:** 82 | **Compression:** ~1:10

---

## Executive Summary

This landmark study by McKinsey and VDMA analyzes 215 European machinery companies to identify strategic options for maintaining competitive advantage. The European machinery industry—employing 2.9M workers and generating EUR 644B in revenues—has performed exceptionally well (7.2% CAGR, ~10% EBIT margin 2010-2014), but faces three transformational challenges requiring strategic response.

### Three Strategic Themes

| Theme | Challenge | Required Response |
|-------|-----------|-------------------|
| **Shifting Growth Patterns** | Traditional market growth slowing; BRIC slowdown | Pursue growth beyond the core: new geographies, horizontal/vertical integration |
| **Increasing Digitization** | Digital disruption threatening traditional models | Develop digital business models; drive operational excellence |
| **Organizational Change** | Need for agility, diversity, new capabilities | Transform organization; acquire digital talent; increase cooperation |

## Chapter 1: Business Model Archetypes (pp. 14-27)

The study identifies **five distinct business model archetypes** based on primary revenue sources:

### Archetype Performance Comparison

| Archetype | % of Respondents | Avg Revenue | EBIT Margin | CAGR (2010-14) | Break-Even | Export % |
|-----------|------------------|-------------|-------------|----------------|------------|----------|
| **Component Specialists** | 32% | EUR 571M | 10.2% | 5.6% | 74% | 57% |
| **Machine Manufacturers** | 25% | EUR 186M | 8.3% | 9.7% | 76% | 77% |
| **Equipment & System Providers** | 16% | EUR 468M | 8.2% | 8.3% | 81% | 76% |
| **Aftersales Providers** | 23% | EUR 1,034M | 10.7% | 4.8% | 77% | 80% |
| **Software/System Providers** | 12% | EUR 514M | **13.5%** | 6.1% | **68%** | 50% |

### Key Performance Insights

1. **Scale Effects**: Companies in EUR 500M–1B revenue range achieve peak profitability (14.2% EBIT). Beyond EUR 1B, complexity costs erode margins to 11.3%.

2. **Break-Even Vulnerability**: Average break-even point is 76%, with 40% of equipment providers above 80%. A typical 20% industry downturn would push 40% of companies into losses.

3. **Vertical Integration Impact** (varies by archetype):
   - Component Specialists: High integration nearly **doubles EBIT** (14.7% at 61-80% value-add)
   - Equipment Providers: High integration **destroys value** (drops to 2.7% EBIT)

4. **Software Advantage**: Software/system providers show lowest break-even (68%) and highest EBIT (13.5%)—most resilient business model.

## Chapter 2: Strategic Themes (pp. 28-57)

### 2.1 Shifting Growth Patterns

**Historical vs. Future Growth Drivers:**

| Growth Driver | Past 5 Years | Next 5 Years | Change |
|--------------|--------------|--------------|--------|
| Growing with market | 30% | 21% | -9 pp |
| Market share gains | 28% | 25% | -3 pp |
| New regional markets | 16% | 19% | +3 pp |
| Horizontal integration | 20% | 26% | **+6 pp** |
| Vertical integration | 6% | 9% | +3 pp |

**Strategic Implication:** Companies must shift from passive market growth to active portfolio transformation through:
- **Geographic expansion**: ASEAN, Africa, Iran (post-sanctions)
- **Horizontal integration**: Broadening product/service offerings
- **Vertical integration**: Moving into services and aftersales

**Growth Mechanisms:**
- Organic growth dominates for market share (80%+)
- M&A and JVs gain importance for growth beyond core (20-23% adoption)
- 42% of companies plan to increase vertical integration by 2020

### 2.2 Increasing Pace of Digitization

**Four Digital Business Model Categories:**

| Model | Description | Implemented | Not Strategic |
|-------|-------------|-------------|---------------|
| **Platform-based** | Facilitating exchanges between parties | 20% | 36% |
| **As-a-Service** | Pay-per-use, converting CAPEX to OPEX | 15% | 44% |
| **IPR-based** | Monetizing intellectual property | 17% | 52% |
| **Data-driven** | Monetizing data and insights | 13% | 46% |

**Digital Revenue Target:** Companies expect digital business models to grow from **3% of revenue (2015) to >10% by 2020**.

**Operational Excellence via Digitization:**

| Value Driver | Implementation | Focus |
|--------------|----------------|-------|
| Customization | 49% | External |
| Time to market | 24% | External |
| Sales | 24% | External |
| Services/aftersales | 19% | External |
| Asset utilization | 13% | Internal |
| Supply/demand match | 10% | Internal |

**Barriers to Digitization:**
1. **Lack of qualified personnel** — 56%
2. **Insufficient business cases** — 38%
3. **Data security concerns** — 29% (44% for software providers)

### 2.3 Organizational Change

**Drivers of Change:**
- New technologies: 44%
- Economic volatility: 45%
- Digitization: 39%
- Shifting growth trajectories: 32%

**Agility Gap:**

| Agility Type | Definition | Implemented |
|--------------|------------|-------------|
| **Operational** | Exploit opportunities within existing model | 75% |
| **Portfolio** | Shift resources between business areas | 56% |
| **Strategic** | Spot and seize new opportunities | 50% |

**Cooperation Patterns:**
- With customers: 72%
- With research facilities: 64%
- With suppliers: 64%
- With competitors: Only 18%

**Critical Skills for Next 5 Years:**
1. Software development: 54%
2. Mechanical engineering: 53%
3. Mechatronic engineering: 52%

## Chapter 4: Priority Action Areas (pp. 64-69)

### Strategic Action Timeline

| Timeframe | Growth | Digitization | Organization |
|-----------|--------|--------------|--------------|
| **Now** | Reassess 5-year planning | Develop digitization strategy | Evaluate current organization |
| **2017-18** | Develop growth strategies (new markets, vertical integration) | Drive 5-10% cost reduction | Design agile organization |
| **2018+** | Execute growth beyond core | Monetize digital business models (>10% revenue) | Drive transformation |

### Industry Improvement Priorities
1. **Agility and flexibility**: 63%
2. **Labor cost competitiveness**: 59%
3. **R&D and innovation**: 53%
4. **Bureaucratic efficiency**: 39%

## Appendix: Archetype-Specific Recommendations (pp. 71-75)

### Component Specialists
- Grow internationally via JVs
- Explore adjacent businesses
- Leverage high vertical integration for margin advantage

### Machine Manufacturers
- Shift from products to services (vertical integration)
- Highest growth potential (9.7% CAGR)
- Develop digital service offerings

### Equipment & Machine System Providers
- Offer customer-specific solutions at competitive cost
- Use modularization/standardization
- **Avoid excessive vertical integration** (destroys margins)

### Aftersales Providers
- Already largest companies (EUR 1,034M avg)
- Focus on digitizing service delivery
- Expand recurring revenue streams

### Software/System Providers
- Highest EBIT (13.5%) and lowest break-even (68%)
- Most resilient to downturns
- Lead digital transformation

## Glossary of Key Terms

| Term | Definition |
|------|------------|
| **CAGR** | Compound Annual Growth Rate |
| **EBIT** | Earnings Before Interest and Tax |
| **ROCE** | Return on Capital Employed |
| **Break-even point** | Revenue level required to cover costs |
| **Net value added** | Enhancement given to product/service (% of revenues) |
| **Vertical integration** | Expanding along value chain (e.g., into services) |
| **Horizontal integration** | Broadening offerings at same value chain level |
| **Industry 4.0** | Digitization of manufacturing through IoT, analytics |
