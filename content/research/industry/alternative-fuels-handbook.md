## Executive Overview

This handbook is the definitive industry reference for using alternative fuels and raw materials (AFR) in cement and lime production. Written by practitioners with 20+ years of project experience, it covers the complete value chain from waste evaluation through RDF production to cement kiln feeding, with detailed economic analysis and practical case studies.

The document is divided into two parts:
- **Part I (Chapters 1-11):** Technical and economic fundamentals
- **Part II (Fact Sheets):** Detailed reviews of specific AFR types (olive residues, poultry litter, scrap tyres, sewage sludge, straw, used oils)

## Part I: Technical Fundamentals

### Chapter 1: History and Overview of Alternative Raw Materials and Fuels (p.27-62)

Alternative fuels in cement have been used for 30+ years, with RDF substituting for fossil fuels especially in rotary kilns. Key distinctions:

- **Alternative Raw Materials (ARM):** Substitute for limestone, clay, sand, iron ore in clinker production
- **Alternative Fuels (AF):** Substitute for coal, petcoke, natural gas for thermal energy

The handbook follows the principle that AF use is NOT waste incineration - it's material and energy recycling in an environmentally controlled industrial process with long residence times and high temperatures.

### Chapter 2: Waste Evaluation (p.63-76)

Systematic approach to evaluating waste streams:
1. Define waste-generating sectors
2. Collect required information (quantities, composition, availability)
3. Quantify and characterize mixed waste
4. Assess fuel-technical properties
5. Apply basic principles for AF use

Key requirement: Waste processing costs must be covered by gate fees, disposal fees, or the value of recyclables/final RDF product.

### Chapter 4: Production of RDF (p.83-132) ⭐ TECHNICAL CORE

Comprehensive guide to RDF production equipment and processes:

**4.1-4.7 Separation Technologies:**
- Drum screens/trommels for size classification
- Ballistic separators for 2D/3D separation
- Air classifiers for light fraction
- Magnetic separators (ferrous)
- Eddy current separators (non-ferrous)
- NIR optical sorters
- Manual picking stations

**4.8 Shredding:**
- Pre-shredders: Reduce to <300mm, robust design for mixed waste
- Final shredders: Reduce to <30mm for kiln feeding
- Critical: Shredder knife maintenance = 40% of total OPEX

**4.9 Pelletising:**
- Densifies fluffy RDF for transport/storage
- Typical pellet size: 20-80mm diameter

**4.10 Drying Technologies:**
- Solar drying (lowest cost, weather dependent)
- Belt dryers (continuous, moderate energy)
- Drum dryers (highest capacity, gas-fired)
- Energy: ~850 kWh heat + 40-80 kWh electric per tonne water evaporated

**4.11 RDF Plant Examples:**

Simple plant configuration:
```
Input → Pre-shredder → Magnetic Separator → Final Shredder → Storage
```

Complex plant configuration:
```
Input → Pre-sort → Pre-shredder → Magnetic Sep → Non-ferrous Sep →
NIR → Air Sep → Final Shredder → Pelletiser → Storage
```

### Chapter 6: Logistics and Storage of RDF (p.165-206)

**Transport options:**
- Truck (most common, 20-25t payload)
- Rail (for long distances)
- Ship (for coastal plants)

**Storage types:**
- Open floor storage (lowest cost, fire risk)
- Enclosed halls (weather protection)
- Silos (highest cost, best control)
- Deep bunkers with discharge systems
- Moving floor systems

**Critical: Fire protection** - AF storage requires sprinklers, temperature monitoring, inert gas systems for silos.

### Chapter 7: Dosing and Feeding of Alternative Fuels (p.207-238)

**Feeding points in cement kiln:**
1. **Main burner:** High CV, fine particle size required
2. **Kiln inlet (riser duct):** Coarse materials, whole tyres
3. **Calciner:** Mid-range materials, most flexible

**Weighing systems:**
- Weigh belt feeders
- Loss-in-weight feeders
- Rotor weigh feeders
- Screw weigh feeders

**Conveying:**
- Pneumatic (for fine, dry materials)
- Mechanical (belt, screw, chain conveyors)
- Pipe conveyors (enclosed, low dust)

### Chapter 8: Influences on Clinker and Lime Production (p.239-286)

**Quality impacts from AF use:**
- Ash composition affects clinker chemistry
- Sulphur/chlorine require process adjustments
- Alkali circulation and buildups
- Refractory wear (especially with high-chlorine fuels)

**CO₂ reduction benefits:**
- Biomass content = carbon neutral
- Coal emits 96 kg CO₂/GJ
- Example: 1.45t straw substitutes 1t coal, avoiding 2.45t CO₂

**Thermal substitution rates:**
- Typical target: 30-60%
- Best practice plants: up to 90%+

### Chapter 9: Contracting Alternative Fuels (p.287-292) ⭐ COMMERCIAL

**Industry structure context:**
- New cement plant: >EUR 150M per Mt/year capacity
- Cement industry: long-term, capital intensive
- Waste industry: short-term contracts, price volatile

**Pricing formula for joint ventures:**
```
P_RDF = P_min + 0.5 × (profits - RDF_operating_costs + fuel_savings)
```

**Contract elements:**

Categories by German Waste Oil Ordinance:
- Category I: Can be regenerated

High calorific value liquid fuel, requires dedicated storage and handling systems.
