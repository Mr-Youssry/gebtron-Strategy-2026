# European Industrial Machinery: 2024-2025 Market Analysis

**Compiled from:** Roland Berger (2025), VDMA Economic Reports (2024-2025)
**Purpose:** Strategic planning (SWOT/PESTEL) and financial model benchmarks
**Date Compiled:** January 2025

---

## Executive Summary

The European industrial machinery sector is navigating a **structural downturn** characterized by weak demand, geopolitical fragmentation, and accelerating digital transformation requirements. After years of growth, 2024-2025 represents a valley before expected recovery in 2026+.

### Key Headlines

| Metric | Value | Trend |
|--------|-------|-------|
| Global machinery turnover (2024) | **€3.26 trillion** | -1.5% YoY |
| Germany production (2025) | **-5%** | Continued decline |
| EU machinery production (H1 2025) | **-3%** | Price-adjusted |
| Recovery expected | **2026** | +1-2% growth |
| Tariff uncertainty impact | **94%** executives delaying investments |

## Source 1: Roland Berger — Industrial Automation 2025

**Published:** March 26, 2025 | **Authors:** Ralph Mair, Steffen Oder (Zurich)

### Market Overview

The industrial automation sector hit a "speed bump" in 2024-2025 due to:
- Cooling investment climate
- Supply chain recalibrations
- Bullwhip inventory effect from prior material shortages
- Reduced capital expenditures by end-market companies

### Growth Forecasts by Segment (2025-2030)

| Segment | 2025 Outlook | CAGR to 2030 | Market Share |
|---------|--------------|--------------|--------------|
| **Hybrid industries** (pharma, MedTech, F&B) | Growing | **Up to 9%** | Fastest growing |
| **Process automation** | Flat to moderate | **6-7%** | ~60% of market |
| **Discrete industries** | Flat | Improving post-2025 | Balance |

### Regional Outlook

| Region | 2025 Outlook | Comment |
|--------|--------------|---------|
| **Germany** | **-9%** | Structural dependency on domestic market |
| **Europe overall** | Underperforming | Recovery lagging other regions |
| **Asia Pacific** | "Most forceful expansion" | Leading recovery |

### Leading vs. Lagging End-Markets

**Growing Sectors:**
- Pharmaceuticals and MedTech
- Food & beverage
- Battery/ex-EV production
- Electronics and data centers
- Mining and metals

**Declining Sectors:**
- Automotive engineering
- Select process industries

### Strategic Priorities for 2025

**Short-Term Actions:**

| Priority | Focus Area |
|----------|------------|
| **Sales Stabilization** | E-commerce excellence, service/spare parts activation, AI-driven pricing |
| **COGS Reduction** | Direct material costs, value chain optimization, plant performance |
| **SG&A Efficiency** | Indirect procurement, AI in R&D, process excellence |

**Medium-to-Long-Term Strategy:**

1. Standardize and modularize components
2. Prepare next-generation manufacturing capabilities
3. Realign product strategy and global production footprint
4. Transition hardware to "cash cow" → expand software-driven value
5. Multichannel, digitally-focused sales transformation

## Source 2: Roland Berger — Geopolitical Uncertainty Impact

**Published:** June 18, 2025 | **Author:** Gareth Hayes (Chicago)
**Methodology:** 150+ executive surveys + 20+ CEO interviews | **Partner:** Manufacturers Alliance

### Executive Survey Findings

| Finding | Percentage |
|---------|-----------|
| Tariff uncertainty delaying investment decisions | **94%** |
| Geopolitical risk slowing/stalling long-term plans | **90%** |
| Workforce challenges expected to worsen (5 years) | **83%** |
| Localization "highly relevant" to operations | **74%** (up from 61% in 2023) |
| Growing emphasis on market-specific product development | **72%** |

### Six Key Manufacturing Trends for 2025

#### 1. Capital-Light Localization
Companies shifting from greenfield investments to:
- Repurposing existing facilities
- Regional partnerships
- Asset-light expansion models

#### 2. Geographic Preference Realignment
**Gaining favor:** Politically stable economies (UK, Japan)
**Losing favor:** Traditional low-cost hubs (China, Mexico)
**Priority shift:** Governance reliability > operational cost savings

#### 3. Digital Acceleration
> "Leading manufacturers are more than **twice as likely** to cite digital transformation as a core part of their response"

Key technologies:
- Digital twins
- Predictive maintenance
- AI-driven planning

#### 4. Product Customization
**72%** of manufacturers emphasizing market-specific product development through:
- Regional product platforms
- Local engineering capabilities

#### 5. Sustainability Under Pressure
- Short-term ESG spending delays
- Companies with embedded sustainability strategies maintaining commitment
- Sustainability as strategic differentiator persists

#### 6. Workforce Development Priority
- Intensified training academies
- Automation investments to address skills gaps

### Success Strategies for Outperformers

Three common approaches among top-performing companies:
1. **Flexibility** built into product portfolios and capacity planning
2. **Supply chain alignment** with regional demand profiles
3. **Technology investment** to reduce disruption vulnerability

### Production & Growth

| Metric | Value | Period |

| Global machinery turnover | **€3.26 trillion** | 2024 |
| Global turnover change | **-1.5%** | 2024 vs 2023 |
| 2026 global projection | **+2%** | Price-adjusted |

### Order Intake Trends (Germany)

| Period | Change | Notes |
|--------|--------|-------|
| April 2025 | -6% | Real terms, YoY |
| May 2025 | Positive | Weak prior-year basis |
| October 2025 | Mixed | Bavaria +12%, NRW +1%, BW stable |
| November 2025 | Slight recovery | Momentum building |

**Pattern:** Foreign orders stronger than domestic; non-euro countries driving growth

| Metric | Value |

| Equipment investment trend (Eurozone) | **Stagnation** |
| Manufacturing investment ratio (2023) | **~3.0%** (down from 3.5% in 2019) |

**Strategy:** Core workforce retention prioritized over layoffs
