# Africa Market Research

> Gebtron's Horizon 3 expansion opportunity — EAAS in Sub-Saharan Africa. 15 countries screened, 6 shortlisted, one recommended first market. This is the evidence base for the Africa entry decision.

*6 documents | 60+ min total reading*

---

## The Punchline

Africa's cement industry ($8.1B, 7.6% CAGR) has an alternative fuels thermal substitution rate below 5% — versus 52% in Europe. **No competitor offers EAAS on the continent.** The IFC has invested EUR 242M in Sococim (Senegal) targeting 70% AF substitution, and Dangote's $1B Sinoma deal for 12 new plants excludes AF systems entirely.

The recommended first market is **Senegal** — CFA Franc (EUR-pegged, zero currency risk), 100% foreign ownership, 6-day company registration, and a cement industry actively spending hundreds of millions on alternative fuels infrastructure.

The recommended sequence: **Senegal (2028) → Cote d'Ivoire (2028-29) → Kenya (2029) → Nigeria (2030+)**.

## EAAS Feasibility in Africa
*⏱ 10 min read*

📌 **Read when:** You're evaluating whether the $1M/year savings proposition holds in African markets, or assessing currency and payment risk.

💡 **Key takeaway:** The EAAS model works — delivered coal costs in Africa ($130-200/t) are higher than MENA, and labor costs are 30-70% lower. The biggest technical risk is waste quality: African MSW is 55-70% organic with high moisture, requiring bio-drying stages.
