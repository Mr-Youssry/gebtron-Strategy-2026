## 1. The Fort Model in Africa

The Fort model, proven via AFW in Alexandria, is the entry playbook. Each Fort is a local engineering office providing year-round presence, turnkey capability, and aftermarket services — exactly what European OEMs refuse to do in Africa.

### Recommended Fort Sequence

| Phase | Timeline | Country | Fort City | First Target Customer | Investment |
|-------|----------|---------|-----------|----------------------|-----------|
| **1** | 2028 | Senegal | Dakar | Sococim (Vicat) | $150-250K setup + $3M EAAS line |
| **2** | 2028-29 | Cote d'Ivoire | Abidjan | CIMAF | Incremental (CFA zone, shared team) |
| **3** | 2029 | Kenya | Nairobi | EA Portland / National Cement | $200-300K setup + JV structuring |
| **4** | 2030+ | Nigeria | Lagos (Lekki FZ) | Dangote | Free zone entity + $3M+ EAAS line |
| **5** | 2030+ | Tanzania | Dar es Salaam | Twiga / Amsons | Extension of Kenya team |

### Why This Sequence

**Phase 1-2 (CFA Zone):** Minimal risk. Same currency, same regulatory framework (WAEMU/ECOWAS), same language (French). Dakar to Abidjan is a natural geographic expansion. One team can serve both markets initially.

**Phase 3 (Kenya):** Different regulatory zone (EAC), different language (English), different currency (KES). Requires separate setup — including a mandatory Kenyan JV partner (30% domestic ownership). But Kenya has the strongest proof point (Bamburi 98% AF) and best governance in East Africa.

**Phase 4-5 (Nigeria + Tanzania):** Largest markets but highest risk. Enter only after proven success in CFA zone and East Africa. Nigeria through a free zone to manage FX and regulatory complexity.

### Fort Staffing (Phase 1 — Senegal)

| Role | Requirement | Source |
|------|-------------|--------|
| **Country Manager** | French-speaking, engineering background, commercial skills | Hire locally in Senegal or from Morocco/Egypt |
| **Process Engineer** | AF/RDF experience, commissioning capability | Transfer from AFW (Alexandria) |
| **Operations Manager** | Waste management experience, local knowledge | Hire locally |
| **Support** | Admin, logistics, driver | Hire locally |
| **Total Phase 1 team** | **4-5 people** | Mix of transferred + local |

**Cost estimate:** $150-250K/year operating cost for a Dakar office (rent, salaries, vehicles, travel).

## 3. The Sococim First Contract Strategy

The most de-risked path to the first Africa EAAS contract:

### Step-by-Step

```
Step 1: Approach Sococim (Vicat Group) through IFC introduction
   ↓ Gebtron positions as RDF production and supply partner
Step 2: Negotiate EAAS terms — EUR-denominated, 10-15 year contract
   ↓ IFC co-financing or credit guarantee de-risks for both parties
Step 3: Build RDF facility near Dakar using Mbeubeuss waste feedstock
   ↓ AFW handles engineering design and procurement
Step 4: Commission and operate — Dakar Fort team runs the facility
   ↓ First 12-18 months prove the model
Step 5: Use Sococim reference to approach CIMAF (Abidjan HQ, 13 plants)
   ↓ "We did it for Sococim with IFC backing — we can do it for you"
Step 6: Expand to Dangote Senegal, then Cote d'Ivoire, then East Africa
```

### Why Sococim First (Not Dangote)

| Factor | Sococim | Dangote |
|--------|---------|---------|
| Scale | Single plant, manageable | 12 plants across 7 countries — overwhelming for first entry |
| Currency | CFA (EUR-pegged) | Multi-currency across 11 countries |
| DFI backing | IFC EUR 242M specifically for AF | IFC $500M to BUA (competitor), not Dangote-specific |
| Decision-making | Vicat Group (France) — structured corporate process | Dangote Group — founder-led, unpredictable |
| Competitive tension | No incumbent AF supplier | Geocycle (Holcim) may compete even after Huaxin acquisition |

**Start with Sococim. Prove the model. Then approach Dangote from strength.**

| **Waste supply** | UCG (Senegal), HYSACAM (Cameroon), Zoomlion (Ghana), LAWMA (Lagos) | MSW supply agreements | Pre-EAAS |
| **DFI financing** | IFC, Proparco (Francophone), AfDB | Concessional debt for EAAS SPVs | 2027-28 |
| **Local JV (Kenya)** | TBD — need partner with waste + cement access | 30% domestic ownership requirement | 2028-29 |
| **Technology** | Fuller Technologies (ex-FLSmidth), GECOS equipment | AF equipment supply chain | Ongoing |

### Dangote Relationship Development

Dangote is the strategic key but requires careful approach:

1. **Do NOT approach Dangote first.** Prove EAAS in Senegal/Cote d'Ivoire.
2. **Build the reference case:** "We supply RDF to Sococim at 70% TSR under IFC-backed EAAS."
3. **Leverage exhibitions:** Dangote sends representatives to major trade shows (Mohamed already has exhibition strategy).
4. **Target the 12-plant gap:** "Your Sinoma deal builds the plants — we supply the AF systems."
5. **Offer EAAS, not equipment:** "Zero CapEx. We build, we operate, you save 25% on fuel."

## 5. Regulatory Quicksheet

| Country | Foreign Ownership | Registration Speed | Equipment Import Duty | Environmental Permit | UAE BIT |
|---------|------------------|-------------------|----------------------|---------------------|---------|
| **Senegal** | 100% | 6 days (APIX) | 5% (exemptions available) | EIA required; IFC precedent | Signed, not ratified |
| **Cote d'Ivoire** | 100% | Days-weeks (CEPICI) | 5% (Investment Code exemptions) | EIA required | **In force** |
| **Kenya** | 70% max foreign | 2-4 weeks | 0-10% (EAC CET) | EIA via NEMA; WtE framework | **In force** |
| **Cameroon** | 100% | 2-4 weeks | 5% (WAEMU CET) | EIA via MINEPDED | Check status |
| **Tanzania** | 100% | 2-4 weeks (TIC) | 0-10% (EAC CET) | EIA via NEMC | Not found |
| **Nigeria** | 100% (most sectors) | 4-8 weeks (triple agency) | 5-10% (Pioneer status for exemptions) | EIA via NESREA | Signed, not ratified |

**Key insight:** Most countries offer **duty-free or reduced-duty import of capital goods** for approved investments. Register with investment agencies (APIX, CEPICI, TIC, GIPC, NIPC) before importing equipment.

## 6. Risk Register

| # | Risk | Probability | Impact | Mitigation | Residual |
|---|------|------------|--------|------------|----------|
| **R1** | Waste quality (high organic/moisture) | High | High | Bio-drying, C&D blending, Super RDF, aged waste mining | Medium |
| **R2** | Currency devaluation (non-CFA) | High | High | Start in CFA zone; USD-index non-CFA contracts | Low (CFA) / Medium |
| **R3** | MSW supply unreliable | Medium | High | Long-term municipality contracts; invest in collection | Medium |
| **R4** | Political instability | Medium | High | Diversify across countries; CFA zone is most stable | Medium |
| **R5** | French language gap | High | Medium | Hire 1-2 French speakers from Egypt/Morocco/locally | Low (once hired) |
| **R6** | Gebtron capacity constraint (WT-3) | High | High | Sequence strictly; no parallel expansion | Medium |
| **R7** | No tipping fee revenue | High | Medium | Revenue model on RDF sales only; explore carbon credits | Medium |
| **R8** | Chinese EPC competition | Medium | Low | Complementary — they build plants, not AF systems | Low |
| **R9** | Cement overcapacity in some markets | Medium | Medium | EAAS reduces costs — more attractive in overcapacity | Low |
| **R10** | Reppie-type technical failure | Low | High | RDF ≠ incineration; quality control at production | Low |
| **R11** | DFI funding unavailable | Low | Medium | Multiple DFIs active; IFC already invested in Sococim AF | Low |
| **R12** | Dangote vertically integrates AF | Low | Medium | Procurement-based AF strategy, not in-house manufacturing | Low |

### The Binding Constraint: WT-3

From the SWOT-TOWS (Draft 09): **"Focused geographic sequencing, not parallel expansion — cannot fight on 5 fronts with a 15-person team."**

Africa is **Horizon 3** — it follows EAAS proof in MENA (H2).

**The non-negotiable sequence:**
1. **2026-27:** Prove EAAS in Egypt/UAE. Build the team. Hire the CFO.
2. **2028:** First Africa Fort (Senegal). One market. One customer. One line.
3. **2028-29:** Expand to Cote d'Ivoire (same zone, low incremental cost).
4. **2029:** Kenya (new zone, new currency, needs JV partner).
5. **2030+:** Nigeria and Tanzania (large markets, proven playbook).

**Violating this sequence — entering Africa before MENA is proven — would be the single biggest strategic mistake.** The strategy paper is right: Africa is 2028+, conditional on EAAS success in Egypt/UAE.
