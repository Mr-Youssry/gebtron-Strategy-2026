# European Legal & Tax Structure

> How a UAE FZCO acquires and holds a European company. The Netherlands BV solves the Germany tax problem.

*⏱ 8 min read*
**Audience:** CEO, Legal Counsel, Tax Advisor
**Date:** April 15, 2026

---

## Can Gebtron FZCO Directly Own a European Company?

**Yes.** There is no legal prohibition against a UAE FZCO holding 100% of a German GmbH, Austrian GmbH, Polish Sp. z o.o., Czech s.r.o., or Portuguese Lda.

But **should** it own directly? Not always — tax treaties matter.

---

## Tax Treaties: UAE ↔ Target Countries

| Country | DTA Status | Dividend WHT (≥10% holding) | Interest WHT | Capital Gains |
|---------|-----------|---------------------------|-------------|---------------|
| **Germany** | **EXPIRED (Dec 2021)** | **~26.375%** | Up to 15% | May be taxable |
| **Austria** | In force (2003) | **0%** | 0% | Exempt |
| **Poland** | In force (2013) | **5%** | 0-5% | Exempt |
| **Czech Republic** | In force (2023) | **5%** | 0% | Exempt |
| **Portugal** | In force (2011) | **5%** | 0-10% | Exempt |
| **Netherlands** | In force | **0-5%** | 0% | Exempt |
| **Cyprus** | In force | **0%** | 0% | Exempt |

**The Germany problem is stark.** Without a DTA, EUR 500K in German GmbH profits would cost EUR 132K in WHT before reaching Gebtron FZCO — every year.

## Recommended Holding Structure

```
Gebtron FZCO (UAE — 0% CIT)
    └── Netherlands BV (participation exemption = 0% tax on dividends received)
            ├── German GmbH (acquisition target — IP, heritage, customers)
            │     └── EU Parent-Subsidiary Directive → 0% WHT to NL BV
            ├── Polish Sp. z o.o. (operations base — warehouse, CE marking)
            │     └── DTA → 5% WHT to NL BV (or 0% via EU P-S Directive)
            └── Future entities (Czech, Portuguese, etc.)
```

### Why Netherlands BV

| Factor | Netherlands BV |
|--------|---------------|
| Participation exemption | **0% tax** on dividends received from subsidiaries (if ≥5% holding) |
| WHT to UAE parent | **0-5%** under NL-UAE DTA (with substance test) |
| EU Parent-Subsidiary Directive | Eliminates WHT on intra-EU dividends |
| Substance requirements | Need real office, local director or management company |
| Setup cost | EUR 3,000-5,000 |
| Annual cost | EUR 5,000-15,000 (accounting, compliance, substance) |
| Credibility | High — Netherlands is a mainstream holding jurisdiction |

### Alternative: Cyprus Ltd (Budget Option)

| Factor | Cyprus Ltd |
|--------|-----------|
| CIT | 12.5% (but participation exemption on dividends = 0% effective) |
| WHT to UAE | **0%** (no WHT on dividends to non-residents) |
| Setup cost | EUR 2,000-4,000 |
| Annual cost | EUR 3,000-8,000 |
| Credibility | Lower than NL — but improving |

### Direct Ownership Works for Austria

Austria has **0% dividend WHT** to UAE under the 2003 DTA. If the only European entity is Austrian, no holding company is needed — Gebtron FZCO can own the Austrian GmbH directly with no tax leakage.

## FDI Screening — Will Gebtron Be Blocked?

**Almost certainly not.** FDI screening in all 5 countries targets sensitive sectors:

| Country | Screened Sectors | Bulk Material Handling? |
|---------|-----------------|----------------------|
| Germany | Defense, critical infrastructure, AI, semiconductors, media, IT security | **No** |
| Austria | Defense, critical infrastructure, water, energy, food, health, media | **No** |
| Poland | Defense, energy, telecom, chemicals, pharma | **No** |
| Czech Republic | Military, critical infrastructure, media, energy | **No** |
| Portugal | No comprehensive FDI screening law | **N/A** |

**Caveat:** If a target company has any defense contracts or dual-use technology, screening may apply in Germany/Austria. For standard industrial/machinery/cement equipment companies — no screening.

## Transfer Pricing

All 5 countries follow OECD Transfer Pricing Guidelines. Key considerations:

| Transaction Type | Risk Level | Notes |
|-----------------|-----------|-------|
| Management fees (Gebtron → subsidiary) | Medium | Must be at arm's length; document the service provided |
| IP royalties | High | If Gebtron licenses technology to subsidiary, pricing is scrutinized |
| Intercompany loans | Medium | Interest rate must be market-rate |
| Equipment sales | Low-Medium | Comparable pricing available in the market |

**Germany has the most stringent documentation requirements** (Aufzeichnungspflichten). Establish a transfer pricing policy before closing the first acquisition.

**Recommendation:** Engage a Big 4 or mid-tier firm (Rodl & Partner has UAE + Germany expertise) to set up the TP framework before the first cross-border transaction.
