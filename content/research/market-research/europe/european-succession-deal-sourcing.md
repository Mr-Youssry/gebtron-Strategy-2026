# European Succession Crisis & Deal Sourcing

> 600,000 German SMEs planning succession. 47% have no successor. Here's where to find them.

*⏱ 12 min read*

## Scale of the Problem

### The Numbers

| Country | Businesses facing succession | No successor identified | Annual closures due to failed succession |
|---------|----------------------------|-----------------------|----------------------------------------|
| **Germany** | **600,000** (next 5 years) | **47%** (~280,000) | ~6,000 viable businesses/year |
| **Austria** | 6,800/year | ~40-45% | Not tracked |
| **Poland** | 830,000 (next decade) | Only 8% have a written plan | Not tracked |
| **Czech Republic** | 50,000+ (by 2030) | First-generation founders aging | Not tracked |
| **Portugal** | ~30% of family SMEs (next 10 years) | Limited data | Not tracked |
| **EU-wide** | **450,000/year** | ~one-third | ~150,000/year |

**Sources:** KfW-Mittelstandspanel, DIHK Nachfolgereport 2024, IfM Bonn, WKO, European Commission SME Strategy 2020, Polish Family Business Institute

### Germany Is the Epicenter

- **KfW:** 560,000-600,000 SME owners planning handover/sale within 5 years (16% of all German SMEs)
- **47% have NOT identified a successor** — worsening from 39% in 2012
- **DIHK:** 28,000-30,000 entrepreneurs contact their local IHK about succession annually — tip of the iceberg
- **IfM Bonn:** ~125,000-150,000 business transfers/year; ~6,000 economically viable businesses close due to failed succession
- **VDMA:** Many of its 3,400+ member companies (mechanical engineering) have owners approaching retirement with no clear successor
- **Average owner age:** Share of owners 55+ grew from 20% (2000) to over 30% (mid-2020s)

### Manufacturing Is Well-Represented

Industry distribution of succession-situation companies (Germany):
- **Manufacturing/production: ~25-30%**
- Skilled trades: ~20-25%
- Wholesale/retail: ~15-20%
- Services: ~15-20%
- Construction: ~10%

**Machinery and industrial equipment manufacturing is core Mittelstand territory.** The 25-30% manufacturing share of succession cases, applied to 600,000 companies, means **150,000-180,000 manufacturing/industrial companies** will change hands in the next 5 years in Germany alone.

## The Search Fund Model

A "search fund" is where an entrepreneur raises capital to search for, acquire, and operate a single SME. Growing rapidly in Europe.

| Metric | Value |
|--------|-------|
| Active European search funds | ~150-200+ (2024) |
| Growth | From <20 in 2010 to 150+ now |
| Top markets | Spain (#1), UK, Germany (growing), France |
| Typical target | EUR 5-15M enterprise value, 20-80 employees |
| Leading institution | IESE Business School (Barcelona) |

**Relevance for Gebtron:** Not a pure search fund — Gebtron is an operating company, not a first-time entrepreneur. But the **search fund methodology** (systematic screening, due diligence frameworks, post-acquisition playbooks) is directly applicable. And search fund investor networks understand the European succession asset class.

**Key programs:**
- IESE Search Fund Center: https://www.iese.edu
- Search Fund Accelerator (London): https://www.searchfundaccelerator.com
- INSEAD ETA Program: https://www.insead.edu
- WHU Otto Beisheim (Germany): Has ETA courses

## Success Stories & Lessons

### What Works (from Chinese Mittelstand acquisitions)

| Factor | Successful Example | Lesson for Gebtron |
|--------|-------------------|-------------------|
| Operational expertise | Weichai → KION/Linde Hydraulics | **Being an operator, not a financial buyer, wins trust** |
| Job preservation | ChemChina → KraussMaffei | Commit to maintaining German jobs and location |
| Cultural respect | Joyson → Preh GmbH (grew post-acquisition) | Preserve company name, brand, management autonomy |
| Long-term horizon | All successful cases | German sellers prefer 10+ year horizon, not PE-style 5-year flip |

### What Fails

| Mistake | Cautionary Example | How Gebtron Avoids It |
|---------|--------------------|-----------------------|
| Political backlash | Midea → KUKA (led to new FDI screening) | Bulk material handling is NOT a sensitive sector |
| IP stripping perception | Various Chinese deals | Gebtron adds capabilities (MENA market access), doesn't strip |
| Cultural mismatch | Hierarchical management vs. Mittelstand consensus | Mohamed's relationship-first approach is culturally aligned |
| Overpaying | Control premiums in competitive auctions | Target succession situations (10-20% discount, bilateral negotiation) |

### The Model That Fits Gebtron Best

**Model 3: "Partnership Then Acquisition"**
1. Start as commercial partner (sales agent, distribution, project collaboration)
2. Build trust and understanding over 1-2 years
3. Propose acquisition when relationship is established

**This is exactly what happened with GECOS/Gebtron** — and it's already happening with Kockele. The succession wave means more companies will be receptive to this approach.
