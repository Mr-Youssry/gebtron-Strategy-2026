# European Expansion Playbook

> How to acquire a European company and where to base operations. The SBA model works in Europe — 10% equity, 80% leverage. Poland is the recommended operations base. Germany has the targets but a tax problem.

*5 documents | 50+ min total reading*

---

## The Punchline

The US SBA acquisition financing model **can be replicated in Europe.** Germany's KfW + Bürgschaftsbank + MBG silent participation structure gives Gebtron 80% leverage with only 10% cash equity — virtually identical to the American model. There are **600,000 German SMEs** currently planning succession, 47% with no identified successor. nexxt-change.org lists 7,000-9,000 businesses at any given time.

For the operations base, **Poland wins on cost** (42% of Germany's operating costs, 1-3 day online company formation, CIT exemptions up to 70% in Special Investment Zones). But there's a critical tax finding: **the UAE-Germany Double Tax Agreement expired in December 2021 and was not renewed** — dividends from a German GmbH to Gebtron FZCO face ~26.375% withholding tax. A Netherlands BV or Cyprus holding company is needed to solve this.

**Recommended approach:** Acquire a German target for IP/heritage/customers. Base operations in Poland for cost efficiency. Route the structure through a Netherlands BV holding for tax optimization.
