# Competitive Landscape: Alternative Fuels in Africa

> The whitespace finding: **zero EAAS competitors across the entire continent.** Sinoma builds plants but not AF systems. Geocycle serves only Holcim plants. European OEMs have no Africa presence.

*⏱ 8 min read*
**Audience:** CEO, Strategy Team, Partnership Discussions
**Date:** April 15, 2026

---

## The Critical Finding

**No international OEM offers an EAAS (Energy-as-a-Service) model for alternative fuels in Africa.**

Africa's AF competitive density is **3-5x lower than MENA.** Most European OEMs have UAE/Egypt offices but almost none have Sub-Saharan presence. The entire continent is served through periodic visits, equipment shipments, and little to no aftermarket support.

## Key Competitor Profiles

### Sinoma/CNBM — The Dominant EPC (But Not a Competitor)

Sinoma is Africa's largest cement plant builder — 286 clinker lines in 84 countries globally. Their February 2026 deal with Dangote ($1B for 12 plants across 7 countries) makes them the de facto African plant construction partner.

**Why Sinoma is not a competitor but an opportunity:**
- Sinoma builds kilns, not alternative fuel systems
- The $1B Dangote deal **explicitly excludes AF equipment**
- Every Sinoma-built plant needs AF systems from someone else
- Gebtron could position as the AF system integrator for Sinoma-built plants

**Sinoma Africa Projects:**
- Dangote: $1B for 12 projects in Nigeria, Ethiopia, Zambia, Zimbabwe, Tanzania, Sierra Leone, Cameroon
- PPC South Africa: 1.5 Mt/yr, $160M
- Bamburi Kenya: 1.6 Mt/yr, $250M
- Ethiopia: $291M clinker line

### Huaxin Cement — The Chinese Operator

Huaxin acquired Lafarge Africa's 83.8% stake from Holcim in 2025, gaining 4 Nigerian plants (10 Mt/yr). They now control ~30 Mt/yr across Africa and build plants in 11 months (Malawi). They use Chinese equipment exclusively and have no AF focus.

**Implication:** Huaxin takes over Holcim plants but likely abandons Holcim's AF/Geocycle programs. Those former Holcim plants (now Huaxin-owned) may need new AF suppliers — an opportunity for Gebtron.

### Geocycle (Holcim) — Captive, Not a Market Competitor

Geocycle is Holcim's waste management arm. In Africa, they operate at:
- Nigeria: Ewekoro plant (now Huaxin-owned — Geocycle status unclear)
- Kenya: Bamburi Athi River (98% AF — Geocycle/Shell partnership)
- Egypt: Various Holcim plants

**Why Geocycle is not a real competitor:**
- Captive to Holcim plants only — does not serve third-party cement companies
- With Huaxin acquiring Lafarge Africa, Geocycle may lose access to former Holcim plants
- Does not offer EAAS to non-Holcim clients

### Fuller Technologies (ex-FLSmidth Cement) — Strategic Uncertainty

FLSmidth sold its entire cement division to Pacific Avenue Capital Partners for EUR 75M (October 2025), rebranded as Fuller Technologies. Their Fuelflex Pyrolyzer is advanced AF technology (55% fossil replacement) but has no Africa installations.

**Opportunity:** Fuller may be open to a partnership model where Gebtron represents their AF technology in Africa. PE ownership typically seeks revenue growth through partnerships.

## The Three Big Opportunities

### 1. Dangote — 12-Plant Pipeline

| Metric | Value |
|--------|-------|
| Africa capacity | 55 Mt/yr (largest in Africa) |
| Countries | 11 |
| AF systems deployed | 15 (11 commissioned in 2024) |
| AF tonnes co-processed | 1.5M+ tonnes |
| TSR range | 10% average; 41.8% in South Africa |
| 2030 target | 20% CO2 reduction |
| **Sinoma deal** | **$1B for 12 plants — AF systems excluded** |

Dangote is spending $1B building 12 new plants that will each need AF systems. They already have corporate commitment to AF (15 systems deployed, 1.5M+ tonnes co-processed). This is a multi-year procurement pipeline worth hundreds of millions in AF equipment and services.

### 2. CIMAF — 13 Plants, Zero AF

CIMAF (Ciments d'Afrique) operates **13 plants across 10 African countries** from its Abidjan headquarters. Every single one has **zero alternative fuel capability.** IFC invested EUR 45M in their Sahel operations.

Countries: Senegal, Cote d'Ivoire, Mali, Burkina Faso, Guinea, Guinea-Bissau, Cameroon, Gabon, Congo, Chad.

**CIMAF represents 13 greenfield AF opportunities in a single commercial relationship.** Starting with Senegal (CIMAF Sahel, IFC-backed) creates the reference for the rest.

### 3. Sococim — The First Contract

Sococim (Vicat Group) in Senegal has:
- IFC-backed EUR 242M decarbonization program
- 70% TSR target (currently ~10-15%)
- Active need for RDF supply partners
- Coal imported from South Africa at $140-165/t

This is the most de-risked path to a first Africa EAAS contract.

## Africa vs. MENA Competitive Density

| Dimension | MENA | Africa |
|-----------|------|--------|
| European OEM offices | 8-10 OEMs with UAE/Egypt offices | 1-2 (Loesche SA; Pfeiffer Egypt/Dubai) |
| AF equipment installations per country | Multiple | Single-digit across entire continent |
| EAAS or managed service models | None (Gebtron would be first) | None |
| Local turnkey AF companies | Emerging (Sinoma, Redecam in Egypt) | None |
| Cement industry TSR | 10-30% in Egypt | <5% continent-wide |
| **Competitive moat for first mover** | **Medium** | **Very High** |

**The first company to establish local AF presence in Sub-Saharan Africa will have a 5-10 year head start.** European OEMs see Africa as too risky/small. Chinese EPCs build plants but not AF. The gap is Gebtron's opportunity.
