## 1. Senegal — The Recommended First Market

### Why Senegal First

Sococim (Vicat Group) has IFC backing (EUR 242M) for a 70% alternative fuels target. They need RDF supply. That is exactly what EAAS provides. The CFA Franc is pegged to the EUR. Registration takes 6 days. No competitor offers EAAS here.

### Cement Industry

| Metric | Value | Confidence |
|--------|-------|------------|
| Key players | Sococim (Vicat), CIMAF, Dangote Cement Senegal | Confirmed |
| Estimated capacity | ~8-10 Mt/yr | Industry estimates |
| Primary fuel | 100% imported coal from South Africa ($140-165/t delivered) | IFC project docs (confirmed) |
| Current TSR | ~10-15% (Sococim leading) | Vicat Group reporting |
| **Target TSR** | **70% at Sococim** (IFC-financed) | IFC project database (confirmed) |
| **IFC investment** | **EUR 242M** to Sococim for decarbonization incl. AF | Confirmed |

**Sococim is the most advanced AF program in West Africa.** They have demand, investment, and corporate backing — but need RDF supply partners. This is Gebtron's EAAS value proposition.

### Waste / MSW

| Metric | Value |
|--------|-------|
| Dakar MSW | 2,500-3,000 t/day (~0.9-1.1M t/yr) |
| Mbeubeuss dumpsite | 175 hectares, 50+ years accumulated waste |
| Collection rate | 60-70% in Dakar |
| Composition | 45-55% organic, 12-16% plastics, 8-10% paper |
| Calorific value (raw MSW) | 7-10 MJ/kg |
| **Data quality** | Low-Moderate (UCG estimates) |

**Volume risk:** Dakar alone is borderline for 800K t/yr. Mitigations: aggregate from Thies/Saint-Louis; mine aged waste from Mbeubeuss (lower moisture, higher caloric); start with 10 t/hr line.

### Entry Requirements

| Requirement | Details |
|-------------|---------|
| Foreign ownership | 100% allowed |
| Registration | APIX one-stop shop, ~6 days |
| Minimum capital | CFA 10M (~EUR 15K) for SA entity |
| Environmental permit | EIA required; IFC-financed AF precedent exists |
| Import duties | 5% for capital goods (WAEMU/ECOWAS CET); exemptions for approved investments |
| UAE BIT | Signed but NOT ratified — may need Mauritius structuring |
| Currency | **CFA Franc (XOF) — pegged to EUR at 655.957** |

### EAAS Economics

| Parameter | Estimate |
|-----------|----------|
| Delivered coal cost | $140-165/t |
| RDF cost advantage vs. coal | 25-35% per GJ |
| Annual savings per EAAS line | $0.8-1.2M |
| Currency risk | **None** |
| Profit repatriation | Free under WAEMU rules |

## 2. Cote d'Ivoire — The CFA Zone Expansion

### Why Cote d'Ivoire Second

Natural expansion from Senegal: same CFA Franc, same ECOWAS/WAEMU framework, larger population (30M), and CIMAF is headquartered in Abidjan with 13 plants across 10 African countries — all with **zero AF capability**. Gebr. Pfeiffer already has a client here (CIMCI — MVR 3070 R-2 for clay grinding).

### Cement Industry

| Metric | Value |
|--------|-------|
| Key players | LafargeHolcim, CIMAF (HQ), CIMCI, Diamond Cement |
| Capacity | ~10-12 Mt/yr |
| Utilization | ~45% (overcapacity — factories under cost pressure) |
| Current TSR | <5% estimated |
| IFC investment | EUR 45M to CIMAF Sahel operations |

**Overcapacity = opportunity.** Factories under cost pressure are more receptive to EAAS ($1M/yr savings, zero CapEx).

### Waste / MSW

| Metric | Value |
|--------|-------|
| Abidjan MSW | 3,000-4,000 t/day (~1.1-1.5M t/yr) |
| Infrastructure | Akouedo dumpsite closed 2019; Kossihouen replacement |
| ANAGED | National waste agency created 2017 |
| **Data quality** | **Low** — limited published waste characterization |

### Entry Requirements

| Requirement | Details |
|-------------|---------|
| Foreign ownership | 100% allowed — no specific approval |
| Registration | CEPICI one-stop shop, online portal (225invest.ci) |
| UAE BIT | **In force** — investment protection available |
| Currency | CFA Franc (XOF) — EUR-pegged |

## 3. Kenya — The East Africa Anchor

### Why Kenya Third

The strongest proof point in Africa: **Bamburi Cement achieved 98% alternative fuel substitution at its Nairobi plant.** Kenya has the best waste management regulatory framework in East Africa, no energy subsidies, highest industrial electricity costs ($0.20+/kWh), and 2.4% excise duty on imported coal (making fossil fuels MORE expensive).

### Cement Industry

| Metric | Value | Confidence |
|--------|-------|------------|
| Manufacturers | 9 | Confirmed |
| Clinker capacity | ~10 Mt/yr + 5 Mt/yr under construction | cemnet.com |
| Grinding capacity | 16 Mt/yr | Confirmed |
| Consumption (2024) | 8.4-9 Mt | Confirmed |
| Q1 2025 growth | +14.7% YoY | ccement.com |
| Bamburi/Sinoma new line | 1.6 Mt/yr clinker, USD 250M, Matuga | Confirmed |
| EA Portland upgrade | USD 200M, capacity to 4 Mt/yr | Confirmed |
| **Bamburi AF rate** | **98%** at Nairobi plant | Confirmed (Holcim/Geocycle reports) |

**The opportunity isn't Bamburi** (they're vertically integrated via Geocycle). It's the **other 8 companies** — EA Portland (just completed $200M upgrade), National Cement, Mombasa Cement, Savanna Cement — none of which have AF capability.

### Waste / MSW

| Metric | Value |
|--------|-------|
| Nairobi MSW | 4,000-5,000 t/day (~1.5-1.8M t/yr) |
| Dandora dumpsite | Largest in East Africa, 30+ acres |
| Regulatory | Best in East Africa — Sustainable Waste Management Act 2022, plastic ban 2017 |
| Private sector | Taka Taka Solutions, Mr. Green Africa (MRFs in Nairobi) |

### Entry Challenge

**30% domestic ownership requirement** for newly registering foreign companies. Gebtron needs a Kenyan JV partner — someone with waste management access AND cement industry relationships.

| Requirement | Details |
|-------------|---------|
| Foreign ownership | Limited — 30% domestic requirement |
| UAE BIT | **In force** |
| Currency | KES — moderate depreciation (5-8%/yr historically) |
| Energy subsidies | **None** — plus excise duty on coal |

## 4. Nigeria — The Whale

### Why Nigeria Later (Not First)

Largest market by every measure: 230M people, 62.8 Mt/yr cement capacity, Lagos generates 5.5M+ tonnes MSW/yr. But also highest risk: volatile NGN (70%+ loss in 2023-24), complex regulation, strong incumbents.

### Cement Industry

| Metric | Value |
|--------|-------|
| Capacity | 62.8 Mt/yr; 70+ Mt/yr post-expansion |
| **Dangote Cement** | 35.25 Mt (58.5% share); Itori 6 Mt greenfield Nov 2026; $1B Sinoma deal for 12 plants |
| **BUA Cement** | 17 Mt (23.6%); IFC $500M investment |
| **Lafarge/Huaxin** | 10.5 Mt (17.9%); acquired by Huaxin from Holcim in 2025 |
| Dangote AF | 15 systems deployed; 11 commissioned in 2024; TSR ~10%; 41.8% in South Africa |

**The Dangote pipeline:** $1B Sinoma deal for 12 new plants **excludes AF systems**. Every one of those plants is a potential EAAS customer. Dangote is already co-processing 1.5M+ tonnes of alternative fuels — they want more.

### Waste / MSW

| Metric | Value |
|--------|-------|
| Lagos MSW | 15,000-18,000 t/day (~5.5-6.5M t/yr) |
| National MSW | 32-35M t/yr |
| LAWMA | Lagos Waste Management Authority — active PPP model |
| Collection rate | 20-30% nationally; ~40% in Lagos |

### Entry Strategy

**Lekki Free Zone** recommended to bypass ownership complexity and manage FX risk. Contracts must be USD-denominated.

| Requirement | Details |
|-------------|---------|
| Minimum capital | NGN 100M (~$62K) |
| Registration | Triple: CAC + NIPC + Business Permit |
| UAE BIT | Signed but **NOT ratified** — Mauritius structuring advisable |
| Currency | **NGN — extreme volatility** |

## 5. Tanzania — The East Africa Extension

### Why Tanzania with Kenya

Second-largest East Africa cement market. 99% capacity utilization (supply constrained). Surplus production exports regionally. Natural extension of Kenya presence.

### Cement Industry

| Metric | Value |
|--------|-------|
| Integrated plants | 6 + several grinding |
| Production (2024) | 10.9 Mt (demand ~8.5 Mt, surplus exported) |
| Key players | Twiga (Heidelberg), Dangote (Mtwara 3 Mt), Amsons (expanding) |
| Expansion | Amsons USD 320M Bagamoyo plant |
| Fuel | Imported coal from South Africa |

### Entry Requirements

| Requirement | Details |
|-------------|---------|
| Foreign ownership | 100% allowed |
| TIC registration | USD 500K minimum for incentives |
| Capital goods import | 0-10% under EAC CET |
| Currency | TZS — moderate risk |

## 6. Cameroon — The Francophone Bridge

### Why Cameroon

Unique advantage: **HYSACAM**, the dominant private waste operator across Francophone Africa, provides a ready-made waste supply partner. CIMENCAM (LafargeHolcim) has corporate AF targets. CFA zone = zero currency risk.

### Key Data

| Metric | Value |
|--------|-------|
| Cement | CIMENCAM (LafargeHolcim), Dangote, CIMAF; some overcapacity |
| Waste | Douala + Yaounde combined: ~2M+ t/yr |
| Waste partner | HYSACAM — quasi-monopoly = single negotiation point |
| Currency | CFA Franc (XAF) — EUR-pegged |
| Registration | Moderate complexity |
