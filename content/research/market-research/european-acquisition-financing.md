# European Acquisition Financing — The SBA Equivalent

> The US model (80% loan, 10% down, 10% deferred) can be replicated in Europe using stacked government programs.

*⏱ 12 min read*
**Audience:** CEO, Finance Team, CFO (when hired)
**Date:** April 15, 2026

---

## The Bottom Line

For a EUR 5M acquisition, the structure is:

```
10%  (EUR 500K)  — Buyer equity (Gebtron)
10%  (EUR 500K)  — Seller note / earn-out (2-3 years)
10%  (EUR 500K)  — MBG silent participation (quasi-equity)
70%  (EUR 3.5M)  — Senior bank loan
                    (KfW-backed + Bürgschaftsbank guarantee at 80%)
```

**This gives Gebtron 80% leverage with only 10% cash equity** — virtually identical to the US SBA model.

## Germany — KfW + Bürgschaftsbank (Best Developed)

### KfW Bank (Kreditanstalt fur Wiederaufbau)

**ERP-Grunderkredit — Universell:**
- Covers business acquisitions and successions explicitly
- Up to **EUR 100M**
- Interest: 1.0-3.5% (subsidized, below market)
- Term: up to 20 years (3 years repayment-free)
- KfW finances up to 100% of eligible investment
- Source: https://www.kfw.de/inlandsfoerderung/Unternehmen/Grunden-Nachfolgen/

**Burgschaftsbanken (Guarantee Banks):**
- Each German state has one — provides **government-backed guarantees up to 80%** of loan
- Maximum: EUR 1.25M (EUR 2.5M in some states via "Burgschaft ohne Bank")
- **This is the closest equivalent to the SBA guarantee mechanism**
- Entrepreneur needs ~10-15% equity
- Source: https://www.buergschaftsbanken.de/

**MBG (Mittelstandische Beteiligungsgesellschaften):**
- Silent equity participations (stille Beteiligungen) of EUR 25K-2.5M
- **Counted as equity** for bank loan purposes — reduces cash needed
- Available in every German state
- Combined with Burgschaftsbank + KfW = the SBA stack

**Foreign buyer eligibility:** The borrowing entity must be a **German-registered GmbH**. Gebtron FZCO sets up or acquires the target GmbH, and that entity applies for KfW financing. Non-EU nationals access KfW indirectly through the German entity.

## Austria — aws (Similar to Germany)

**aws Ubernahme-Forderung (Takeover Promotion):**
- Specifically targets business successions
- Guarantees up to **80% of loan**
- Maximum: EUR 2.5M
- Plus coaching grants (up to EUR 5,000) for succession planning
- Source: https://www.aws.at/foerderungen/aws-uebernahmefoerderung/

**aws ERP-Kredit:**
- Low-interest loans from ERP Fund (Marshall Plan origin, same as Germany)
- Up to **EUR 30M**
- Interest: 0.5-1.5% below market
- Term: up to 15 years

**Tax advantage:** No inheritance/gift tax in Austria since 2008. Goodwill amortizable over 15 years.

## Poland — BGK + ARP + PFR

**BGK (Bank Gospodarstwa Krajowego) Guarantees:**
- Up to **80% of bank loans**
- Maximum: PLN 3.5M (~EUR 800K) under de minimis; larger under COSME/InvestEU
- Source: https://www.bgk.pl/

**ARP (Industrial Development Agency):**
- Loans and equity for industrial companies
- Targets manufacturing succession and restructuring
- PLN 1-50M
- Source: https://arp.pl/

**PFR (Polish Development Fund):**
- Mezzanine and equity co-investments for SME acquisitions
- Covers 10-25% of deal as quasi-equity
- Source: https://pfr.pl/

**Cost advantage:** Polish targets valued at **20-30% lower multiples** than comparable German businesses.

## Czech Republic — CMZRB

**CMZRB Guarantee Programs:**
- Guarantees up to **80% of bank loans**
- Maximum: CZK 40M (~EUR 1.6M)
- Fee: 0.1-0.3% p.a.
- Source: https://www.cmzrb.cz/

**EXPANZE Program:**
- Interest-free or low-interest loans up to CZK 60M (~EUR 2.4M)
- EU Structural Fund-backed

**CzechInvest incentives:**
- For manufacturing investments over CZK 50M: **up to 10-year corporate tax holiday**
- Source: https://www.czechinvest.org/

---

## Portugal — Banco Portugues de Fomento

**SPGM Mutual Guarantee Societies:**
- Guarantees up to **75-80% of loans**
- Maximum: EUR 1.5M per operation
- Four regional societies: Norgarante, Lisgarante, Garval, Agrogarante

**Banco Portugues de Fomento (Development Bank):**
- Created 2020, modeled after KfW
- Co-investment, guarantees, credit lines for SMEs
- Manages InvestEU Portugal window

**Golden Visa:** Investing EUR 500K+ in a Portuguese company can provide residency — easing management of the acquisition.

## Deal Economics — What to Expect

### Valuation Multiples (Revenue EUR 5-30M Industrial/Manufacturing)

| Metric | Range | Typical |
|--------|-------|---------|
| EV/Revenue | 0.5x-1.5x | 0.7x-1.0x |
| EV/EBITDA | 4.0x-8.0x | **5.0x-6.5x** |
| EV/EBIT | 6.0x-10.0x | 7.0x-8.5x |

**Succession discount:** 10-20% off market multiples (no competitive auction). "Distressed succession" (health/urgency): 20-30% discount.

**Country adjustment:**
- Germany: top of range
- Austria: ~5-10% below Germany
- Poland/Czech: **20-30% below Germany**
- Portugal: 15-25% below Germany

### Transaction Costs (for a EUR 5M deal)

| Item | Germany | Poland | Czech |
|------|---------|--------|-------|
| Legal fees | EUR 30-100K | EUR 15-50K | EUR 15-50K |
| Notary | 0.5-1.5% | 0.1-0.5% | 0.1-0.3% |
| Due diligence | EUR 30-80K | EUR 15-40K | EUR 15-40K |
| M&A broker | 3-6% | 3-5% | 3-5% |
| **Total** | **4-10%** | **3-7%** | **3-7%** |

**Budget EUR 200-500K in transaction costs for a EUR 5M deal.**

### Timeline: LOI to Closing

| Phase | Duration |
|-------|----------|
| Contact → NDA → Info Memo | 2-4 weeks |
| Valuation → LOI | 2-4 weeks |
| Due diligence | 6-12 weeks |
| SPA negotiation | 4-8 weeks |
| Financing (KfW) | 4-6 weeks |
| Notarization + closing | 1-2 weeks |
| **Total** | **3-6 months** |

## Gebtron Target Profile

| Criterion | Specification |
|-----------|---------------|
| Revenue | EUR 2-15M (sweet spot: EUR 3-8M) |
| EBITDA | EUR 0.5-2M |
| Employees | 15-80 |
| Industry | Machinery, industrial equipment, bulk material handling, weighing, conveying, cement technology |
| Owner age | 60+ with no identified successor |
| Location | Germany (preferred), Austria, Poland, Czech Republic |
| IP/Heritage | Established product lines, customer relationships, brand recognition |
| Expected deal value | **EUR 2.5-10M** |
| Gebtron equity needed | **EUR 250K-1M** (10% of deal) |
