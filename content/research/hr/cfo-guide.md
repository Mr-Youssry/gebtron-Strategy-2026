# CFO Guide — Role Design, Responsibilities & Compensation

> **Read time:** 15 min | **Audience:** Mohamed Youssef, Ahmed Youssry
>
> **Read when:** You're planning the CFO hire, defining the role scope, or discussing whether to go full-time vs. fractional.
>
> **Key takeaway:** Gebtron doesn't need a traditional CFO — it needs a **project finance architect** who can structure EAAS deals, build DFI relationships, and create the financial infrastructure for a company transforming from equipment seller to energy services provider. The role pays for itself with the first EAAS contract.

> [!warning] DRAFT
> This document is a working draft. Compensation data is based on market research as of April 2026. All figures should be validated before making offers.

---

## 1. Why This Role Is Critical

The CFO hire appears in **three TOWS quadrants** — more than any other single action item in the strategy:

| TOWS Code | Strategy | Why CFO Is Central |
|-----------|----------|--------------------|
| **WO-1** | Hire CFO with project finance experience | Cannot structure EAAS deals without financial leadership |
| **WT-5** | Build financial function before EAAS requires it | If a downturn hits while launching EAAS without a CFO, we fail twice |
| **WT-1** | Build management depth before crisis | CFO reduces key-person risk on Mohamed |

Mohamed's own words:

> "I have a problem — I don't have a CFO. Mohamed [the accountant] is more of an accounting person. And he has [experience] but doesn't have financial vision at all. So that's something we need to work on — in reporting, in everything really. I'm running without a CFO right now."

### What's Blocked Without a CFO

| Initiative | How It's Blocked |
|------------|-----------------|
| **EAAS first contract** | No one can structure the SPV, negotiate with lenders, or model the 15-year cash flows for a client presentation |
| **DFI relationships** | IFC, AfDB, and Proparco won't engage with a company that has no finance function |
| **European acquisition** | Due diligence, deal structuring, and KfW loan applications require financial sophistication |
| **Investor readiness** | No financial reporting, no dashboards, no projections — cannot raise capital |
| **Project profitability** | Currently "opaque" — no one knows which projects make money |

## 2. Role Design — Three Options

### Option A: Full-Time CFO (Ideal — When Revenue Supports It)

**When:** Revenue exceeds $2-3M combined (Gebtron + AFW) or first EAAS contract is signed.

**Profile:**
- 10-15 years experience in industrial/infrastructure finance
- Project finance background (SPVs, concession agreements, DFI relationships)
- Has structured deals in emerging markets (MENA, Africa preferred)
- Arabic + English fluent; German/French a plus
- Comfortable in a small, founder-led company (not a corporate bureaucrat)

**Reporting:** Directly to Mohamed. Sits on the leadership team alongside the future Head of Engineering and Regional Sales Manager.

### Option B: Fractional CFO (Recommended — Start Now)

**When:** Immediately. Gebtron's current revenue (~$700K from FZCO alone) cannot justify a full-time senior finance hire, but the strategic need is urgent.

**Profile:**
- Part-time (2-3 days/week) senior finance professional
- Same project finance and emerging market background as Option A
- Available through CFO-as-a-Service firms or independent consultants
- Can be UAE-based (for proximity) or remote with monthly visits

**Engagement model:**
- 6-12 month initial contract
- Specific deliverables: EAAS financial model validation, SPV legal template, first DFI introduction, financial reporting framework
- Converts to full-time if EAAS launches and revenue grows
- Cost: 40-60% of a full-time CFO

**Why this is the recommendation:** It puts financial capability in place within weeks rather than the 5-6 months a full-time hire takes. The fractional CFO's first deliverable — a bankable EAAS financial model — could be worth $15M in contract value over 15 years.

### Option C: Upgraded Financial Controller + External Advisors

**When:** If budget is extremely tight and EAAS timeline slips to 2027+.

**Profile:**
- Hire a strong Financial Controller (more affordable than CFO)
- Supplement with external advisors for specific tasks (SPV structuring, DFI introductions)
- Mohamed retains strategic finance decision-making

**Risk:** This perpetuates the key-person dependency. Mohamed remains the de facto CFO, stretched across strategy, sales, engineering, and now finance.

## 3. Responsibilities — Phased Rollout

### Phase 1: Foundation (Months 1-3)

**Priority: See the numbers clearly.**

| Responsibility | Deliverable |
|---------------|-------------|
| **Financial reporting framework** | Monthly P&L, balance sheet, cash flow — consolidated across Gebtron FZCO + AFW |
| **Project profitability analysis** | Margin analysis for every active and recent project. Which make money, which don't? |
| **Cash flow forecasting** | 12-month rolling cash flow model with scenario planning |
| **QuickBooks / ERP alignment** | Ensure UDO ERP (in progress) and QuickBooks produce reliable, timely data |
| **Budget creation** | First formal annual budget for Gebtron + AFW combined |

### Phase 2: EAAS Financial Architecture (Months 3-6)

**Priority: Make EAAS bankable.**

| Responsibility | Deliverable |
|---------------|-------------|
| **EAAS financial model validation** | Review and stress-test the EAAS model (v2/v3). Validate assumptions on RDF pricing, coal substitution rates, transportation costs, and contract economics |
| **SPV legal structure** | Design the Special Purpose Vehicle template for EAAS contracts — ownership structure, risk allocation, profit distribution |
| **DFI relationship building** | Open conversations with IFC, AfDB, Proparco, KfW/DEG. Position Gebtron as a credible EAAS partner |
| **Client financial presentation** | Create the bankable pitch: "Here's how you save $1M/year with zero CapEx" — with auditable numbers |
| **Risk quantification** | Currency risk, MSW supply risk, counterparty risk — quantified, not just listed |

### Phase 3: Growth Finance (Months 6-12)

**Priority: Fund the transformation.**

| Responsibility | Deliverable |
|---------------|-------------|
| **European acquisition finance** | Structure the acquisition of a European company using KfW + Bürgschaftsbank model (10% equity, 80% leverage) |
| **EAAS project finance** | Secure debt financing for the first EAAS installation (~$3M per 20 t/h line) |
| **Investor readiness** | If pre-IPO funding is pursued, prepare data room, financial projections, and investment memorandum |
| **ESOP financial design** | Model the ESOP's financial impact: dilution, option pricing, vesting schedule economics |
| **Africa market entry finance** | Structure the financial model for CFA zone EAAS deployment (Senegal/CdI as first markets) |
| **Tax optimization** | Ensure the holding structure (UAE → Europe → operating entities) is tax-efficient given the expired UAE-Germany DTA |

### Phase 4: Strategic Finance (Year 2+)

**Priority: Build the institution.**

| Responsibility | Deliverable |
|---------------|-------------|
| **Multi-entity financial consolidation** | As Forts open (Riyadh, Africa), consolidate all entities into group reporting |
| **IPO preparation** | If the Dubai stock exchange path is pursued, build the financial infrastructure for listing |
| **Treasury management** | Manage currency exposure across EGP, AED, USD, EUR, and CFA Franc |
| **Board reporting** | As governance formalizes, provide board-quality financial reporting |

## 4. Candidate Profile — What to Look For

### Must-Have

| Requirement | Why |
|-------------|-----|
| **Project finance / infrastructure experience** | EAAS is a project finance product, not an equipment sale. The CFO must think in SPVs, concession models, and 15-year cash flow projections |
| **Emerging market experience** | DFIs, currency risk, political risk, local regulatory navigation — not learnable from textbooks |
| **Arabic + English fluency** | Must communicate with Egyptian team, Gulf clients, and international lenders |
| **Comfort with ambiguity** | This is a founder-led company with ~$700K revenue aspiring to transform its entire business model. Not for someone who needs corporate structure |
| **Hands-on willingness** | Will initially be the entire finance function. Must be willing to do the accounting review AND the DFI pitch |

### Strong Preferences

| Preference | Why |
|------------|-----|
| **Industrial/energy sector background** | Understands cement economics, energy pricing, waste-to-energy |
| **DFI network** | Existing relationships at IFC, AfDB, or similar institutions |
| **German or French language** | Useful for European acquisition and CFA zone Africa expansion |
| **UAE residency** | Simplifies logistics for a Dubai-headquartered company |
| **Experience at $5-50M revenue companies** | Understands the constraints of a growing SME, not a corporate playbook |

### Red Flags

| Red Flag | Why It's Disqualifying |
|----------|----------------------|
| **Only corporate experience** | Will try to build a finance department Gebtron can't afford. Needs someone who can do it alone |
| **No project finance background** | EAAS requires project finance thinking. Traditional accounting or corporate finance won't work |
| **Expects large team** | The CFO will initially be a team of one. Must be comfortable executing, not just directing |
| **Risk-averse to the point of paralysis** | Gebtron is making bold bets (EAAS, Africa, European acquisition). The CFO must quantify risk, not avoid it |
| **Cannot work remotely/flexibly** | The company operates across Dubai, Alexandria, client sites, and trade shows |

## 5. Compensation Benchmarks

### Egypt (Alexandria / Cairo)

| Role | Monthly Gross (EGP) | Annual Gross (EGP) | USD Equivalent |
|------|--------------------|--------------------|----------------|
| **CFO — Industrial SME (50+ employees)** | 80,000 - 180,000 | 960,000 - 2,160,000 | $19,200 - $43,200 |
| **Head of Finance / Controller** | 45,000 - 100,000 | 540,000 - 1,200,000 | $10,800 - $24,000 |
| **Project Finance Manager** | 60,000 - 130,000 | 720,000 - 1,560,000 | $14,400 - $31,200 |
| **Fractional CFO (2-3 days/week)** | 40,000 - 90,000 | 480,000 - 1,080,000 | $9,600 - $21,600 |

**Egypt market notes:**
- EGP devaluation (from ~30 to ~50/USD between 2023-2024) has compressed USD-equivalent salaries by ~40% — Egyptian finance talent is exceptionally affordable in dollar terms
- Alexandria commands 15-25% lower salaries than Cairo for equivalent roles
- The CFO talent pool in Alexandria is very thin — Cairo-based candidates (ex-CIB, ex-AAIB, ex-Big 4) willing to commute or work hybrid are more realistic
- Project finance specialists are rare; most work at banks or DFIs (IFC, EBRD) in Cairo where they earn significantly more
- A bilingual Arabic/English candidate with DFI experience commands a 20-35% premium over standard CFO rates
- Salary expectations rising 15-25% annually in EGP terms (inflation adjustment), but still declining in USD
- Offering even 1-3% equity would be highly differentiated — virtually no Egyptian SMEs offer ESOP

**Sources:** Bayt.com MENA salary data; Hays Middle East Salary Guide 2025; Glassdoor Egypt; Wuzzuf.net active listings; Mercer Egypt Total Remuneration Survey 2025

### UAE (Dubai)

| Role | Monthly Gross (AED) | Annual Package (AED) | USD Equivalent |
|------|--------------------|--------------------|----------------|
| **CFO — SME / Trading Company** | 30,000 - 65,000 | 360,000 - 780,000 | $98,000 - $212,000 |
| **CFO — Industrial / Free Zone** | 35,000 - 80,000 | 420,000 - 960,000 | $114,000 - $261,000 |
| **Head of Finance / Controller** | 20,000 - 45,000 | 240,000 - 540,000 | $65,000 - $147,000 |
| **Project Finance Manager** | 25,000 - 55,000 | 300,000 - 660,000 | $82,000 - $180,000 |
| **Fractional CFO (2-3 days/week)** | 15,000 - 35,000 | 180,000 - 420,000 | $49,000 - $114,000 |

**UAE market notes:**
- No income tax — gross = net (significant advantage over Germany)
- Total package includes housing allowance (typically 30-40% of base), annual flights, medical insurance
- Employer burden above salary: ~15-20% (visa processing AED 5-7K, mandatory medical AED 5-15K/yr, DMCC card AED 2.5K/yr, end-of-service gratuity 21 days/yr)
- Free zone companies (like Gebtron FZCO) may offer slightly lower packages than mainland companies
- Corporate Tax (introduced June 2023, 9% on profits above AED 375K) has increased demand for senior finance talent
- Project finance specialists are available due to Dubai's infrastructure and energy project ecosystem

**CFO-as-a-Service providers in UAE:**
- **Virtuzone CFO Services** — packages from ~AED 5,000/month
- **Kreston Menon** — mid-tier audit firm with outsourced CFO services
- **Al Dhaheri Jones & Clark** — part-time CFO placements
- **Big 4 SME advisory** — KPMG, Deloitte from AED 15-25K/month
- **Independent fractional CFOs** — significant pool of ex-Big 4 / ex-corporate professionals in Dubai

**Best fit for Gebtron's size:** Fractional CFO at AED 7,000-10,000/month ($23-33K/year) for 3-4 days/month. Scales up as revenue grows.

**Sources:** Robert Half Middle East Salary Guide 2025; GulfTalent.com; Michael Page UAE Salary Benchmark; Hays UAE; Cooper Fitch UAE Salary Guide 2025

### Germany

| Role | Annual Gross (EUR) | USD Equivalent | Total Cost to Employer |
|------|-------------------|----------------|----------------------|
| **CFO — Mittelstand (EUR 2-10M revenue)** | 90,000 - 160,000 | $97,000 - $173,000 | EUR 120,000 - 215,000 |
| **Head of Finance / Controller** | 65,000 - 110,000 | $70,000 - $119,000 | EUR 87,000 - 148,000 |
| **Project Finance Manager** | 75,000 - 130,000 | $81,000 - $140,000 | EUR 100,000 - 175,000 |
| **Part-time Geschäftsführer (Finance)** | 50,000 - 90,000 | $54,000 - $97,000 | EUR 67,000 - 121,000 |

**Germany market notes:**
- Employer social security contributions add ~20-22% on top of gross (health ~7.3%, pension ~9.3%, unemployment ~1.3%, long-term care ~1.7%), capped at ~EUR 90,600/yr
- Corporate tax at 30% + expired UAE-Germany DTA means a German-based CFO is the most expensive option
- However, if Gebtron acquires a German entity, having a German-speaking CFO who can manage the acquisition and local entity is invaluable
- Mittelstand CFOs often wear multiple hats (finance + HR + legal) — a familiar model for Gebtron
- Part-time "teilzeit Geschäftsführer" is well-established and culturally accepted in Germany
- Interim Management market is mature — firms like **Atreus**, **UNITEDINTERIM**, **Butterflymanager** place interim CFOs at EUR 1,200-2,000/day
- German labor law (Kündigungsschutz) makes termination difficult — consider a 2-year fixed-term contract (befristeter Vertrag) initially

**Sources:** Kienbaum Vergütungsreport 2025; StepStone Gehaltsreport 2025; Robert Half Germany; Hays Germany; DDIM Interim Management Report 2025

### The Egypt Arbitrage Opportunity

The EGP devaluation has created a significant pricing window. A CFO-caliber finance professional in Egypt costs $15-30K/year — roughly the same as a junior accountant in Dubai or a part-time bookkeeper in Germany.

**Recommended dual-hire structure:**

| Role | Location | Annual Cost (USD) |
|------|----------|-------------------|
| Head of Finance (full-time) | Egypt (Alexandria/Cairo) | $10,000 - $18,000 |
| Fractional CFO (3-4 days/month) | UAE (Dubai) | $23,000 - $33,000 |
| **Combined** | | **$33,000 - $51,000** |

This gives Gebtron CFO-grade coverage across both entities for **70-80% less** than a single full-time CFO in Dubai. The Egypt-based person handles daily accounting, AFW financial management, and QuickBooks. The Dubai-based fractional CFO handles strategic finance, EAAS structuring, corporate tax, and DFI relationships.

## 6. Recommended Hiring Strategy

### Immediate (Next 30 Days)

| Action | Detail |
|--------|--------|
| **Engage a fractional CFO in UAE** | 2-3 days/week, AED 15,000-25,000/month. Start with a 6-month contract focused on EAAS financial model validation and DFI outreach |
| **Source via:** | CFO Hub Dubai, DIFC-based consulting firms, or Egyptian finance professionals already in UAE (dual culture advantage) |

### Short-Term (3-6 Months)

| Action | Detail |
|--------|--------|
| **Validate the fractional CFO** | Can this person deliver the EAAS financial architecture? If yes, discuss full-time conversion |
| **Build the financial stack** | QuickBooks cleanup → monthly reporting → project profitability dashboard → cash flow forecasting |
| **First DFI meeting** | The fractional CFO's first strategic deliverable should be an introductory meeting with IFC or AfDB |

### Medium-Term (6-12 Months)

| Action | Detail |
|--------|--------|
| **Decide full-time vs. fractional** | If EAAS contract is signed, convert to full-time. If EAAS delayed, keep fractional |
| **Budget for full-time CFO** | Target AED 30,000-45,000/month if UAE-based, or EGP 100,000-150,000 if Egypt-based with regular UAE travel |
| **European finance capability** | If acquisition proceeds, consider a Germany-based financial controller for the European entity (separate from group CFO) |

### The ROI Argument

| Scenario | Without CFO | With CFO |
|----------|-------------|----------|
| **EAAS first contract** | Cannot structure; delayed indefinitely | Structured and presented within 6 months |
| **EAAS contract value** | $0 | ~$2M/year × 15 years = $30M total |
| **DFI financing** | No access | Relationships opened; concessional terms possible |
| **Project profitability** | Unknown — some projects may be losing money | Transparent; unprofitable projects identified and fixed |
| **European acquisition** | Cannot execute due diligence or structure deal | Deal structured using KfW model (10% equity) |
| **Fractional CFO cost** | — | ~$50K-$100K/year |

The fractional CFO costs less than the margin on a single medium project. The full-time CFO costs less than 5% of the first year of EAAS revenue. **This role pays for itself immediately.**

## 7. Interview Framework

### Round 1: Technical Screen (Ahmed)

| Question | What You're Testing |
|----------|-------------------|
| "Walk me through how you'd structure a 15-year energy services SPV in the UAE" | Project finance thinking, SPV mechanics |
| "Gebtron has $3M to invest in an RDF feeding line. How would you finance it?" | Creative structuring — equity, debt, DFI, blended |
| "How would you approach IFC for EAAS financing in Senegal?" | DFI experience and emerging market comfort |
| "Our Egypt entity uses EGP, our HQ uses AED, and EAAS contracts may be in USD or EUR. How do you manage that?" | Multi-currency treasury management |

### Round 2: Culture Fit (Mohamed)

| Question | What You're Testing |
|----------|-------------------|
| "Tell me about a time you worked in a company with less than 20 people" | Comfort with small, founder-led environment |
| "How do you feel about being the only finance person?" | Self-sufficiency, hands-on willingness |
| "What would you do in your first 30 days?" | Prioritization, initiative |
| "How do you explain complex financial structures to non-finance people?" | Communication — Mohamed needs to understand every deal |

### Round 3: Case Study (Joint)

Present the EAAS model and ask the candidate to:
1. Identify the top 3 financial risks
2. Propose a financing structure
3. Draft a 1-page financial summary for a cement factory CEO
4. Suggest how to approach a DFI
