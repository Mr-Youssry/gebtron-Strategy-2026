## 1. Employee Evaluation System

### 1.1 Hiring Assessment: PEAK Performance

P&G evaluates candidates using the **PEAK Performance Assessment**, which measures five core attributes:

| Factor | Description |
|--------|-------------|
| **Lead with Courage** | Willingness to take risks and make difficult decisions |
| **Innovate for Growth** | Creative problem-solving and forward thinking |
| **Champion Productivity** | Efficiency and resource optimization |
| **Execute with Excellence** | Delivering results consistently |
| **Bring Out Your Best** | Personal development and helping others grow |

**Assessment Components:**
- **Leadership Questionnaire** - Assesses past behaviors, drive, attitude, and interpersonal skills
- **Situational Judgment Test** - ~20 minutes, tests decision-making
- **Interactive Assessment** - 30-minute game-based cognitive challenges

Source: [P&G PEAK Performance Assessment](https://www.pgcareers.com/global/en/peak-performance-assessment)

---

### 1.2 Annual Incentive: STAR Program

The **Short-Term Achievement Reward (STAR)** program provides annual cash bonuses based on performance.

**Formula Components:**
```
STAR Award = Base Salary × Target % × (Business Unit Results + Corporate Performance)
```

**Target Percentages by Level:**

| Level | STAR Target % |
|-------|---------------|
| Entry-level | ~8% |
| Mid-level | ~15-40% |
| Senior | ~50-100% |
| Executive | Up to 170% |

**Key Rules:**
- If your level changes during the year, the **highest band level and salary** is used
- Awards tied to both individual business unit AND overall P&G corporate performance

Source: [P&G STAR Program - Justia](https://contracts.justia.com/companies/procter-gamble-1075/contract/866692/)

**My assumption:** P&G likely uses some form of comparative evaluation (ranking employees against peers), even if not formally called "forced ranking." This is typical for companies with pyramid structures and internal promotion cultures.

## 4. Equity Programs: PST/ESOP and LTIP

### 4.1 Overview: Two Main Programs

```
┌─────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────┐
│                           P&G EQUITY COMPENSATION                                   │
├─────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────┤
│                                                                                     │
│   ┌─────────────────────────────────┐    ┌─────────────────────────────────┐       │
│   │     PST & ESOP PLAN             │    │     LTIP                        │       │
│   │     (All Employees)             │    │     (Eligible Employees)        │       │
│   ├─────────────────────────────────┤    ├─────────────────────────────────┤       │
│   │ • Company-funded (since 2007)   │    │ • Stock Options AND/OR RSUs     │       │
│   │ • Common Stock + Preferred      │    │ • Employee chooses mix          │       │
│   │ • Retirement-focused            │    │ • Annual election required      │       │
│   │ • 4-year vesting                │    │ • 3-year vest (RSU) / 10yr (Opt)│       │
│   │ • Age 50 diversification        │    │ • Retention-focused             │       │
│   └─────────────────────────────────┘    └─────────────────────────────────┘       │
│                                                                                     │
└─────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────┘
```

### 4.2 PST & ESOP Plan (Profit Sharing Trust)

**Eligibility:** Substantially all domestic employees

**How It Works:**
- P&G contributes annually (July 1 - June 30) in the form of:
  - **Common Stock** - allocated at market price on purchase date
  - **Preferred Stock** - Convertible Class A Series A, fixed cost basis of $6.82/share

**Preferred Stock Features:**
- Has voting rights (unusual for preferred stock)
- Each share convertible to 1 share of common stock
- Favorable tax basis ($6.82) for future planning

**Vesting:**
- **Fully vested** after 4 years of service + 1,000 hours in 5th year
- **Immediate vesting** at age 65, disability, or death

**Age 50 Diversification Rule:**
- Once age 50, can diversify holdings
- **Must keep at least 40%** in P&G stock to use other investment options

Source: [Vaultis - P&G Retirement Plans](https://vaultis.com/procterandgamble/pgretirementplans), [Schmitt Wealth Advisers](https://schmittwealthadvisers.com/pg-stock-nuances-you-need-to-know/)

---

### 4.3 LTIP (Long-Term Incentive Program)

**Purpose:** Reward contributions and align interests with shareholders

**Award Structure:**
- Denominated in **cash value** (set by P&G), then converted to shares
- Employee chooses mix of Stock Options and RSUs

**Five Election Tiers:**

| Tier | Stock Options | RSUs | Risk/Reward Profile |
|------|---------------|------|---------------------|
| **1** | 100% | 0% | High risk, high upside |
| **2** | 75% | 25% | Growth-oriented |
| **3** | 50% | 50% | Balanced |
| **4** | 25% | 75% | Stability-oriented |
| **5** | 0% | 100% | Low risk, guaranteed value |

**Stock Options vs. RSUs:**

| Feature | Stock Options | RSUs |
|---------|---------------|------|
| **Value** | Only if stock rises above grant price | Guaranteed (if stock has any value) |
| **Vesting** | Exercisable years 3-10 | Vest at year 3 |
| **Dividends** | None until exercised | Dividend equivalents during vesting |
| **Tax Event** | At exercise (spread taxed) | At vesting (ordinary income) |
| **Risk** | Can be worthless | Always has value |

**Annual Election Process:**
- **Window:** Early August
- **Grant Date:** October
- **Important:** Elections do NOT carry forward - must actively choose each year via Executive Compensation portal

Source: [Vaultis - P&G LTIP](https://vaultis.com/procterandgamble/ltip)

---

### 4.4 PST Restoration Program

**Purpose:** Compensate for IRS caps on retirement contributions

**How It Works:**
- IRS limits salary that can be used to calculate PST credits
- This program "restores" the difference between capped credit and what would have been credited without cap
- Applies to all qualifying participants, including CEO

Source: [Vaultis - P&G Retirement Plans](https://vaultis.com/procterandgamble/pgretirementplans)

### 4.5 Strategic Use of Equity for Retention

**⚠️ ASSUMPTION: How P&G Uses Equity Strategically**

Based on the program structures, my assumption on strategic intent:

| Career Stage | Primary Equity Tool | Strategic Purpose |
|--------------|--------------------|--------------------|
| **Entry (Band I)** | PST/ESOP only | Build ownership mindset early |
| **Mid-level (Band II-III)** | PST + LTIP eligibility begins | Retain high performers with vesting handcuffs |
| **Senior (Band III+)** | Higher LTIP grants, more RSU options | Golden handcuffs; significant wealth accumulation |
| **Executive** | Large LTIP + PST Restoration | Align with shareholder returns; major retention tool |

**The "Vesting Ladder" Effect:**
```
Year 1: LTIP Grant 1 given
Year 2: LTIP Grant 2 given
Year 3: Grant 1 vests, Grant 3 given
Year 4: Grant 2 vests, Grant 4 given
...

Result: Always have unvested equity → always a reason to stay
```

## 5. P&G Ventures & Alumni Network

### 5.1 P&G Ventures (Corporate Startup Studio)

**Key Facts:**
- **Founded:** 2015, Cincinnati
- **Type:** Internal startup studio (NOT an investment fund)
- **Mission:** Create new brands in new spaces outside P&G's current categories

> "The goal for P&G Ventures is not to invest for a rate of return but to build a brand with an entrepreneur that 'will be sustainable, global, that will be noticeably superior.'"

**Focus Areas:**
- Works OUTSIDE P&G's current categories
- Seeks next $1B brands that could fold into P&G portfolio
- Partners with entrepreneurs to co-build (not just invest)

**Brands Created:**
| Brand | Category |
|-------|----------|
| **Zevo** | Natural insecticide |
| **Opte** | Digital skin analyzer |
| **Metaderm** | Skin care treatment |
| **Pepper & Wits** | Menopause products |
| **Kindra** | Menopause (with M13) |
| **Bodwell** | Sensitive skin care |

**Partnerships:**
- **M13 Launchpad** (2019) - Spun off multiple startups
- **Disruption Now Media** - Support entrepreneurs of color
- **Revitalize Venture Studio** - Support entrepreneurs of color

Source: [P&G Ventures Studio](https://pgventuresstudio.com/About), [Digiday](https://digiday.com/marketing/brands-start-dtc-insid-procter-gambles-start-brand-studio-pg-ventures/), [Crunchbase](https://www.crunchbase.com/organization/procter-gamble-ventures-studio)

### 5.2 P&G Alumni Network (Independent Nonprofit)

**Key Facts:**

### 5.3 Alumni Startup Support: The Catapult Program

**What It Is:**
- Annual program selecting **25 alumni-led startups**
- Pre-funding clinics to master fundraising
- Top 3 get extra support

**Support Provided:**
- Pitch deck preparation
- Business plan assessment
- Financial modeling
- Risk evaluation

**PGAN Catalyst (Investment Platform):**
- Connects alumni startups with alumni investors
- Uses online investment platforms
- Minimum investment: **$100**
- Opens "Entrepreneur Stars" programs for capital raising

Source: [LinkedIn - PGAN Catapult](https://www.linkedin.com/posts/p&g-alumni-network_catapultprogram-innovation-entrepreneurship-activity-7105550292187787265-HNZa), [LinkedIn - PGAN Catalyst](https://www.linkedin.com/posts/p&g-alumni-network_angelinvesting-startups-pgancatapult-activity-7110627899317043201-CNNM)

### 5.4 Does P&G Invest in Alumni Startups?

**Answer: No (corporate) / Yes (alumni network)**

| Entity | Invests in Alumni? | Details |
|--------|-------------------|---------|
| **P&G Corporate** | No | P&G Ventures builds brands internally, does not invest in external companies |
| **P&G Ventures** | No | Partners with entrepreneurs but doesn't invest for returns |
| **P&G Alumni Network** | Yes | Independent nonprofit runs Catapult & Catalyst programs |
| **Individual P&G Alumni** | Yes | Many alumni become angel investors in other alumni's startups |

**⚠️ ASSUMPTION: Why the Separation?**

My assumption on why P&G doesn't directly invest in alumni startups:
1. **Liability** - Investing creates fiduciary obligations
2. **Conflicts** - Alumni startups might compete with P&G
3. **Focus** - P&G Ventures is about building P&G-owned brands
4. **Independence** - Alumni network can operate without corporate bureaucracy
